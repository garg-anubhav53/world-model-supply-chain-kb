# Neo Performance Materials (TSX: NEO / OTC: NOPMF) — Wave 3 Causal Diligence

**Date:** 2026-07-05 · **Verdict: KILL (already re-rated) — thesis intact but the asymmetry is gone**

Ex-China sintered NdFeB magnets (Narva, Estonia) + rare-earth separation incl. Dy/Tb. The causal story is real and among the cleanest in the group. The problem is entirely one of entry price: the stock has already done the thing we hunt for *before* we arrived.

---

## STEP 0 — Elimination gate

**(a) Financial health — PASS, but with a scar.**
- Dec 31, 2025: cash **$38.4M**, gross debt **$101.8M**. Down hard from $77.3M cash / $68.4M debt at Q1 2025.
- FY2025 operating cash flow: **−$54.0M**. BUT the drivers are largely non-structural: strategic inventory build (geopolitical hedging; inventories surged to ~$205M), receivables timing, and a one-time **$12.5M European patent settlement** (Mar 2025). Not a broken-economics burn.
- FY2025 revenue $478.8M (~flat YoY). Capex $23.3M (Narva build), now normalizing to ~$8-11M/yr sustaining+growth.
- **Q1 2026 inflection:** revenue **$155M (+27% YoY)**, adjusted EBITDA **$36.2M** (doubled from $17.1M), EPS $0.34 (beat 84%). FY2026 EBITDA guide **raised to $100-110M** (from $75-80M).
- Thin cash ($38M) against a Narva ramp + potential Phase 1b is the one genuine watch item, but EBITDA is now clearly funding the business. Not distressed.

**(b) Management execution — PASS (strong).** Built Narva in <2 years, opened Sep 2025, shipping qualification samples on schedule, PPAP H1 2026, commercial H2 2026. Also stood up Europe's first Dy/Tb oxide line. Suleman has delivered.

**(c) Re-rating grade vs MP/Lynas — FAIL for our purposes.**
- Stock ~**C$37.46** (Jul 4 2026), **+97% over 6 months**, outperforming TSX by ~97pts. Analyst targets marched from ~C$26 → ~C$44. Market cap ~C$1.52B.
- Neo is now the *earnings-quality* name of the trio (positive, doubling EBITDA vs MP's negative TTM EBITDA and Lynas's ~83x). That is exactly why it re-rated: the market has already awarded it the "cheap, profitable Western processor with a magnet plant" narrative.
- We hunt names that have **NOT** run and have a physically-necessary link. Neo has the link but has emphatically **run**. Gate (c) is the kill.

**GATE RESULT: technically passes (a)+(b), fails our "not-run" screen at (c). → KILL.**

## 1. Causal chain — rating: **PROBABLE** (one of the strongest in the group, but distinguish demand from Neo-specific capture)

- Demand certainty: **airtight.** Robots/EV/AV traction motors need high-temp NdFeB; China ~90% processing + 98-99% of separated Dy/Tb + MOFCOM No.18 licensing + EU CRMA legally force non-China sourcing. Ex-China heavy-RE prices run **4-6x** Chinese domestic.
- Neo-specific capture: **probable, not airtight.** Named demand exists: a **European Tier-1 EV traction-motor supplier** contract (~$50M, ~35% of Narva finished capacity, deliveries mid-2026 → 2033, peak 2029), plus **Bosch MoU** and **Schaeffler** named as customers. This is a genuine forced-sourcing capture — Narva is the first integrated (oxide→magnet) NdFeB plant in Europe, fed by Neo's own Silmet separation. That vertical integration is a real moat vs. import-dependent rivals.
- Discount: it's still mostly **qualification/sample stage**. Real commercial offtake is H2 2026+. Competing Western capacity (MP 10X, USA Rare Earth Stillwater, Noveon) is scaling into the same demand. So Neo captures *a* slice, not the whole Western deficit.

## 2. Multiplication math — **YES, it multiplies (2028-29), but timing is far and it's priced**

- Base: FY2025 rev $478.8M; FY2026 EBITDA guide $100-110M.
- Narva Phase 1a = **2,000 tpa** = 2.0M kg. Finished ex-China high-temp sintered NdFeB realistically ~$70-100/kg (ex-China premium) → **~$140-200M incremental revenue** at full utilization.
- Phase 1b to **5,000 tpa** → **~$350-500M** — i.e. roughly **doubles** total company revenue, and at magnet-level (not oxide-level) margins. Plus the Dy/Tb line sells into the extreme 4-6x ex-China premium.
- So this is a **revenue multiplier, not just "add a plant"** — Narva at 5,000t ≈ current whole-company revenue. BUT: (i) full run-rate is ~2028-2029; (ii) the C$37 stock and C$44 targets already discount much of it.
- **RE price risk flagged:** NdPr ran +138% YTD to ~$126/kg (Apr) then corrected −21% to ~$100 (May), recovering to ~$110 (Jun). DoD's $110/kg MP floor acts as a soft **ceiling** too. Neo is a processor/magnet maker (shovel-agnostic on feedstock) so it is less directionally exposed to RE price than a miner, but margins still ride the spread.

## 3. Red-team

**(i) Vertical integration — MODERATE, and largely NOT a bypass of Neo.** Evidence in 2026 says automakers/robot makers are **NOT** building in-house NdFeB plants from scratch. The prevailing model is exactly what Neo is doing: OEMs lock dedicated capacity via partnerships/offtake with specialist producers (Bosch/Schaeffler→Neo; Apple/GM/DoD→MP). So vertical integration mostly *feeds* Neo's order book rather than bypassing it. The genuine bypass risk is **rare-earth-free motors** (ZF ESM eco, Renault E7A due 2027) — but those are 4-7 years from mass deployment. Net: a real long-tail threat to the whole magnet complex, not a near-term Neo killer.

**(ii) IP/licensing speculation — CLEARED.** Upside is **magnet ORDERS** (physical tonnes at named offtakes), not speculative process-tech licensing. Confirmed.

**New risk A — China de-escalation / RE price collapse.** NdPr already showed a −21% profit-taking correction. A US-China thaw or the **November 2026 MOFCOM export-control review** lapsing/loosening would compress the ex-China premium that underwrites Narva's magnet economics and the Dy/Tb line. Heavies are more insulated (license-gated), lights less so. Asymmetric to the downside for a name already priced for scarcity.

**New risk B — ramp/execution + thin balance sheet.** $38.4M cash, qualification-stage plant, PPAP not yet through, commercial revenue H2 2026. Any Tier-1 qualification slip, yield problem, or Phase 1b funding need (equity raise → dilution) hits a stock already trading near consensus targets. Low margin of safety at C$37.

---

## Bottom line
Clean causal thesis, strong management, genuine revenue-multiplier optionality in Narva — but the re-rating we hunt for has **already happened** (+97% / 6mo, targets to C$44, EBITDA guide doubled). This violates the core "un-crowded, not-yet-run" mandate. **KILL** on entry price, not on quality. Revisit only on a meaningful pullback (RE-price-driven derate, ramp stumble, or Phase 1b dilution) that restores asymmetry.
