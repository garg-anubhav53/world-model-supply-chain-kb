## 1. Causal Chain — SPECULATIVE

- Physics is real: BLDC/PMSM joint motors in humanoids DO need precision silicon-steel stator/rotor laminations, and Mitsui is a genuine top-5 global lamination stamper with high-precision interlock IP. So the *capability* link is airtight.
- BUT the *demand* link to Mitsui specifically is speculative: (i) Mitsui's book is overwhelmingly EV traction motors — well-understood, and softening; (ii) no confirmed humanoid lamination order; (iii) content-per-robot is a rounding error vs. content-per-EV. An EV traction stack is a large, heavy core (tens of kW). A humanoid uses ~20-40 tiny actuator motors with minuscule laminations — total silicon-steel mass per robot is a small fraction of one EV. Even at scale, robot lamination TAM is immaterial against a ¥215B EV-driven base for years.
- **Rating: SPECULATIVE.**

## 2. Multiplication Math — NO
