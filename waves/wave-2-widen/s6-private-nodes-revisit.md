## Executive Summary

Seven nodes were re-examined. Three yielded publicly-accessible small/micro-cap vehicles with genuine forced-spend exposure. The remaining four nodes are confirmed to have no viable listed vehicle at the time of this sweep.

## Node-by-Node Findings

### 1. Teleoperation Rigs / Haptic Teleop Hardware
**Verdict: NO viable listed pure-play.**

The active vendors — HaptX, Force Dimension, Sanctuary AI's internal rigs — are all private. Immersion Corp (IMMR, Nasdaq) holds haptics IP and licenses it to automotive/consumer electronics but is NOT a robotics teleoperation play; its FY25 headline revenue ($1.56B) is entirely inflated by a Barnes & Noble Education consolidation, and the company has received Nasdaq delinquency notices for late 10-Q filings through early 2026. IMMR is excluded.

The closest motion/inertial-sensor name, Movella/Xsens (MVLA OTC), delisted from Nasdaq April 2024 and is now a deregistered OTC shell trading at ~$0.002 with a $20K market cap; its operating business was spun into a private LP. Not investable.

**No accessible public vehicle for this node.**

### 2. Tactile / E-Skin Sensors for Robot Hands
**Verdict: NO viable listed pure-play.**

The three leading vendors — Ensuring Technology (Shenzhen Bay, January 2025 founding), XELA Robotics (Waseda University spin-off, FSR Angel-backed), and SynTouch — are all private. The neuromorphic tactile sensor market is ~$95M in 2025, heading toward $520M by 2034 (21.5% CAGR). The supply side remains entirely venture-backed with no current IPO pipeline visible on any exchange (AIM, TSX, ASX, TSE Growth).

**No accessible public vehicle for this node.**

### 3. Motion Capture Beyond Oxford Metrics/Vicon
**Verdict: ONE candidate — Kudan Inc. (TSE Growth: 4425, Japan)**

While traditional optical/inertial mocap hardware vendors (OptiTrack/NaturalPoint, Qualisys, Xsens/Movella) are all private, the *software* layer of robot-relevant motion capture — SLAM (Simultaneous Localization and Mapping) and artificial-perception software for cameras and 3D sensors — has a listed vehicle.

**Kudan Inc. (TSE Growth: 4425)**
- Pure-play SLAM/AP software; ships perception software that mathematically processes camera + LiDAR data in real time, outputting 3D reconstruction and kinematic maps. Direct consumption by autonomy stacks in AVs, drones, robots.
- FY guidance flags large-scale robotics and digital twin projects expected to drive material revenue; holds ¥3B cash buffer, no near-term dilution need.
- Stock: ~¥1,020/share as of last trade (-37% past 12 months, underperforming Nikkei 225 by -47%). Analyst consensus target ¥3,500 (+243%). Market cap roughly ¥5–7B (sub-$50M USD).
- Access for US investors: purchasable via Interactive Brokers Japan desk, Fidelity International, or ADR-style OTC. Not China-linked; pure Japanese domicile.
- Risk: single-product, loss-making, small TSE Growth listing with thin liquidity. Revenue ramp tied to customer closings on robotics/digital twin contracts, which can be lumpy and delayed.

### 4. Data Annotation / Data-Tooling / Synthetic Data for Robotics & AV
**Verdict: ONE candidate — Appen Ltd. (ASX: APX)**

Scale AI (private, $29B valuation after Meta's $14.3B stake), Labelbox, Encord, SuperAnnotate, and all synthetic-data-for-robotics vendors (SKY ENGINE AI, Vivid3D, Labellerr) are private. TELUS International (NYSE/TSX: TIXT) and TaskUs (NASDAQ: TASK) are mid-cap BPO players, not micro-cap forced-spend names.

**Appen Ltd. (ASX: APX)**
- Listed data-annotation and AI-training services company; collects, labels, and enriches training data for AI models at scale. Directly exposed to the robotics / physical-AI forced-spend wave: management reports 50,000+ units of robotics annotation data delivered, reinforcement learning environments built across 20 domains, and multi-phase program extensions from AI-lab clients.
- FY2025 revenue: A$233M (flat YoY including legacy Google exposure); ex-Google segment improved. FY2026 guidance: $270–$300M revenue with 5–10% EBITDA margin — first positive EBITDA guidance in several years.
- Stock: A$1.33 as of February 2026 pre-market reference; stock was down ~80% from 2021 peak entering 2025 but has begun recovering on robotics-data demand narrative. Market cap ~A$120–150M (roughly $75–100M USD) — genuine micro-cap on a revenue base that forced-spend could inflect.
- Access for US investors: OTC ADR via APXYY, or direct on ASX via international brokerage. No China exposure; Australian-domiciled.
- Key risk: project-based revenue is lumpy and H2-skewed; single-quarter strength (Q4 FY25 at $41.4M, 24.6% EBITDA margin) does not yet confirm trend. Google revenue tail-off was structural and is now largely lapped. Watchout for re-acceleration failing to materialize in H1 FY26.

### 5. Robot Simulation Software
**Verdict: NO viable listed pure-play.**

The simulation stack is dominated by NVIDIA Isaac Sim (mega-cap, not investable for this screen), RoboDK (private, Czech-Canadian), Coppelia Robotics/CoppeliaSim (private), MuJoCo (acquired by Google/DeepMind), and Webots (open-source). No dedicated robot simulation software company is listed on any exchange at small/micro-cap scale.

Dassault Systèmes (EPA: DSY) owns DELMIA and Simpack but is a large-cap ($20B+). MathWorks (MATLAB/Simulink) is private.

**No accessible public vehicle for this node.**

### 6. AV Sensor-Cleaning Systems / Remote Ops
**Verdict: NO viable listed pure-play.**

The vendors in AV sensor-cleaning — Solero Technologies, Actasys, ascencioné, ARaymond, IAV, dlhBOWLES/ABC Technologies (Tier 1 sub), Kautex (Textron subsidiary), Valeo (large-cap) — are all either private startups or captive Tier 1 divisions. No standalone small-cap listed vehicle exists.

Remote-ops platforms (Ottopia, Phantom AI, Designated Driver) are similarly private/captive.

### 7. Cross-Track Small-Base Supplier: Robots + AV + World-Model Data Collection
**Verdict: ONE candidate — indie Semiconductor (Nasdaq: INDI)**

This is the most interesting cross-track find. indie Semiconductor designs mixed-signal/analog chips for ADAS, radar, LiDAR-enabling vision, and automotive electrification. As of 2025, the company explicitly commenced supply to humanoid robotics market leaders (first cameras shipping in humanoid robot applications), while simultaneously serving automotive radar/vision Tier 1s. This dual exposure — automotive ADAS + humanoid robotics sensor chips — is the cross-track pattern.

- Revenue: $217.4M FY2025 (+0.3% YoY); Q1 2026 revenue $54.1M (+3.3% YoY). Strategic backlog: $7.4B. Non-GAAP gross margins ~49–50%.
- Robotics catalyst: a Gen8 radar product from a Tier 1 partner incorporating INDI's chipset entered production in Q3 2025; humanoid robotics customers actively in ramp.
- Loss-making: FY2025 net loss -$143M; quarterly losses $31–39M. Path to profitability dependent on automotive content-per-vehicle ramp plus robotics volume. Not for the impatient.
- Market cap: ~$800M–1.1B USD (borderline small-cap, not micro-cap). Already partially re-rated from robotics narrative. Flag as "already moving."
- Access: Nasdaq-listed, straightforward for US investors. No China revenue concentration flagged.
- Risk: remains deeply unprofitable; any automotive volume shortfall collapses the growth story; humanoid robotics ramp could slip 12–18 months.

## Coverage Gaps

The following nodes were re-examined thoroughly and confirmed to have **no accessible, investable listed vehicle** at micro/small-cap scale as of July 2026:

1. **Teleoperation rigs / haptic teleop hardware** — all vendors private (HaptX, Force Dimension); IMMR is a governance-troubled haptics licensor with BNED consolidation noise, not a robotics play; MVLA is a worthless OTC shell.
2. **Tactile / e-skin sensors** — all leading vendors (Ensuring Technology, XELA Robotics, SynTouch) are private; market too early for listed pure-plays; estimated $95M TAM in 2025 is too small to support a listed vehicle.
3. **Robot simulation software** — dominated by NVIDIA (too large), private companies (RoboDK, Coppelia), or open-source (MuJoCo, Webots). No independent listed small-cap exists.
4. **AV sensor-cleaning systems** — all vendors are private startups or captive Tier 1 divisions (Kautex/Textron, Valeo, dlhBOWLES/ABC Technologies). No standalone listed name.

These gaps are genuine — they are not oversights but structural features of where the supply chain currently sits (pre-IPO, captive, or embedded in large-cap Tier 1s).
