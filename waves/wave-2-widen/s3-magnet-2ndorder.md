# S3: Magnet & Heavy Rare Earth Chain — Second-Order Names
**Slice:** NdFeB magnet makers ex-China, heavy rare earth (Dy/Tb) suppliers, magnet recyclers, separation/processing small-caps  
**Date:** 2026-07-05  
**Status:** 7 searches exhausted; see Coverage Gaps

---

## Macro Frame

China's April 2025 export controls on 7 medium/heavy rare earths (Dy, Tb, Gd, Sc, Lu, Y, Er), NdFeB magnets containing them, and November 2025 expansion (Ho, Er, Tm, Eu, Yb + equipment for milling/separation/refining) created a structural pricing wedge: by March 2026 dysprosium oxide was ~$317/kg FOB China vs. $190/kg domestic; terbium ~$1,182/kg vs. $804/kg. A January 1, 2027 U.S. ban on Chinese-origin NdFeB in defense systems creates a hard deadline. Structural supply shortfall expected through 2027-2028 at minimum (Macquarie forecasts sustained NdPr deficit with prices rising into 2026). This forces Western OEMs (robotics, EV, defense, wind) to qualify ex-China supply chains — mandatory spend, not discretionary.

The obvious re-rated names (MP, Lynas, Novanta) are already covered or flagged. The question is what is *next* in the chain.

## Candidate Profiles

### 1. Neo Performance Materials (TSX: NEO) — CORE PICK
**What:** The only integrated ex-China rare earth company operating at scale in the West: rare earth separation at Sillamäe (EU's only commercial RE separation plant), NdFeB alloy, and now the first sintered NdFeB magnet plant in Europe (Narva, Estonia — opened Sept 2025, 2,000 tpa capacity for EV/wind/industrial). Also has the first EU dysprosium/terbium oxide production line. Supplies Schaeffler, Bosch.  
**Why small-base & forced-spend:** Revenue was ~C$476M in 2024 (down from $572M on weaker pricing), but the Narva magnet plant didn't contribute — it opened Sept 2025. A 2,000 tpa sintered magnet plant at ~$50-100/kg ASP adds $100-200M incremental revenue at maturity. The $50M EV traction motor supply contract (deliveries from mid-2026) is the first of what should be many EU OEM qualifications. The upstream Dy/Tb line at Sillamäe is the only ex-China source of these oxides in Europe — forced spend for EU automakers under CRMA 2030 targets.  
**Rough move:** BMO raised to C$38; Stifel raised to C$38 from C$26. Stock up ~58%/year over 3 years on a price/earnings basis. EPS beat Q1 2026 by 79% (C$0.47 vs. C$0.26). **But** analyst targets are C$38; the stock is still described as "undervalued" at ~C$28 (implied further ~35% upside to target). The re-rating is underway but likely incomplete given the Narva ramp just starting.  
**Accessibility:** TSX listed, no China JV risk. US investors can access via OTC (NOPMF). Small-cap by global standards.  
**Risk:** Revenue -17% in 2024 suggests price/volume sensitivity; Narva ramp execution; EU auto demand softness.

### 2. Mkango Resources / HyProMag (TSX: MKA; HyProMag not separately listed)
**What:** Mkango holds ~78% of Maginito, which owns HyProMag — pioneer of HPMS (Hydrogen Processing of Magnet Scrap), the short-loop magnet recycling technology that produces near-net-shape recycled NdFeB without full chemical reprocessing.  
- UK plant (Tyseley Energy Park, Birmingham): commercial-scale HPMS vessel commissioned July 2025; UK's first commercial-scale rare earth magnet recycling and manufacturing plant.  
- German plant (HyProMag GmbH, Pforzheim): first commissioning runs completed 2026, licensed for 750 tpa NdFeB output, initial ~100 tpa rising to ~350 tpa phased; full capacity under evaluation.  
- EXIM letters of intent for $92M for HyProMag USA (Texas facility).  
- HyProMag USA strategic review for potential separate U.S. listing late 2026/early 2027.  
- Mkango Rare Earths SPAC deal (Crown PropTech / CPTK): NASDAQ listing of Songwe Hill + Pulawy RE projects signed July 2025, still pending.  
**Why small-base & forced-spend:** HPMS is a licensed proprietary process — uniquely positioned for EU CRMA "sustainable sourcing" requirements and supply chain due diligence. Recycled NdFeB qualifies as ex-China. Mkango is pre-revenue at the parent level (revenue comes as HyProMag scales). If HyProMag USA lists separately, there is a potential re-rating event.  
**Rough move:** Stock remains a micro-cap (Mkango on TSX). The SPAC/NASDAQ uplift and HyProMag USA IPO are the catalysts; current trading likely has not priced full HyProMag Texas scale.  
**Accessibility:** TSX listed (MKA). Also OTC accessible for US investors. Flag: complex holding structure (Mkango → Maginito → HyProMag). Monitor the NASDAQ uplift catalyst.  
**Risk:** Pre-revenue; technology de-risked at UK scale but US execution unproven; SPAC/uplift timing uncertain.

### 3. Ionic Rare Earths (ASX: IXR)
**What:** Two-pronged: (1) Makuutu ionic clay rare earth project in Uganda (very high heavy REE content — Dy/Tb rich); (2) Ionic Technologies (Belfast) — first Western producer of separated magnet rare earth oxides from recycled materials (commercial-scale, unlike peers who produce powder alloys). The Belfast facility produces separated NdPr, Dy, Tb oxides from recycled end-of-life magnets — a critical distinction because separation is the hard part.  
**Why small-base & forced-spend:** Market cap ~A$68M (March 2026) — tiny. The Makuutu project is ionic-adsorption clay style (lower capex, faster timeline than hard rock), heavily weighted toward heavy rare earths (Dy/Tb) which are the most constrained post-export-controls. The Belfast recycling facility is the *only* Western entity producing separated heavy REE oxides from recycled feedstock at commercial scale — plugs directly into magnet makers' qualification needs.  
**Rough move:** 52-week range 0.15 AUD to 1.05 AUD as of March 2026; last at ~0.31 AUD (vs. 1.05 peak — down ~70% from peak). Did run on export-control spike but has since retraced substantially. May offer re-entry if Uganda project advances and Belfast gains qualifications.  
**Accessibility:** ASX listed; US OTC accessible (IERRF). No China operations.  
**Risk:** Uganda political risk; small team; project financing risk for Makuutu; already had one large spike + retrace.

## Thematic Overlay

The **convergence of four mandates** creates forced spend:
1. EU Critical Raw Materials Act (CRMA) — 10% domestic, 40% EU-processed by 2030
2. U.S. defense ban on Chinese NdFeB (Jan 1, 2027 — hard deadline)
3. DOD Direct Investment (MP, USAR) setting a floor on domestic supply price
4. Automotive OEM qualification requirements (18-24 month lead times) — qualifications happening *now* for 2027-2028 production

**The specific gap:** There is NO single ex-China company that can supply separated Dy/Tb oxides, make NdFeB alloy, sinter magnets, and deliver at automotive scale. Neo Performance Materials is the closest to covering the full chain in one entity. Ionic Technologies (IXR subsidiary) and HyProMag are the only recycling-based separated output sources. This gap is the alpha; every OEM must close it.

## Coverage Gaps

The 7-search budget leaves the following areas under-researched:

1. **Sintering/magnetizing equipment makers** — No public small-cap found that makes sintering furnaces, magnetizers, or GBD (grain-boundary diffusion) equipment specifically for NdFeB. Japanese/German private equipment makers dominate; this segment may not have accessible public pure-plays. Candidates to investigate: Tokin, Daido Steel, Shin-Etsu (large-cap, already re-rated), TDK (large-cap). Smaller GBD technology IP holders not surfaced.

2. **Specialty magnet coatings** — Not surfaced. NdFeB requires corrosion-resistant coatings (Ni, Al, epoxy, Parylene). No public small-cap coating specialist found in searches. May be a private or division-level play.

3. **Korean magnet makers** — JS Link (Korean, small, signed Lynas MOU) mentioned but no ticker confirmed or financials found. Other Korean NdFeB producers (e.g., Semes-affiliated, Samsung SDI adjacencies) not researched.

4. **Japanese second-tier magnet chain** — TDK, Shin-Etsu, Daido already re-rated. Smaller Japanese magnet/alloy makers not researched within budget.

5. **Grain-boundary diffusion (GBD) technology holders** — Reduces Dy/Tb use by 30-50% while maintaining coercivity. Key IP mostly in Chinese hands (BGRIMM) or large Japanese labs. No accessible small-cap public pure-play found. Worth a dedicated search.

6. **European processing — Solvay La Rochelle** — Confirmed commercial production of RE oxides for magnets in April 2025 + U.S. supply contracts. But Solvay is a large-cap Belgian chemical company; rare earth is a small segment. Track whether they spin out or form a dedicated JV.

7. **Recycled feedstock aggregators** — Companies that collect, sort, and pre-process end-of-life magnets (from motors, HDDs, wind turbines) for recyclers. This logistics layer is likely private.
