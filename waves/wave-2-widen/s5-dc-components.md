# Slice 5 — Data-Center Liquid-Cooling & Power-Delivery Components
*World-Model / Robotics / AV Supply Chain — Wave-2 Widening*

## Thesis Frame

The obvious data-center infrastructure layer — Vertiv, GE Vernova, Modine, nVent, Eaton, Quanta — re-rated violently in 2024–2025. This slice goes one level deeper: the **components inside** the cooling systems and power-delivery stacks that those OEMs are now scrambling to source at scale. The forced-spend dynamic is structural: single AI training racks now draw 120–150 kW vs. 10–15 kW for a standard 2023 server rack — a 10x step-change in heat density that mandates liquid cooling and high-density power delivery. OEMs cannot ship without the parts. Small-revenue component makers get pulled up by orders of magnitude larger demand.

Key product categories in scope:

**Liquid Cooling:** cold plates, coolant-distribution unit (CDU) internals, quick-disconnect (QD) couplings, manifolds, thermal-interface materials (TIMs), dielectric/immersion coolants, pumps.

**Power Delivery:** power modules / VRMs, multi-layer ceramic capacitors (MLCCs), inductors/magnetics, busbars, high-current connectors, solid-state transformers, gate-driver ICs.

## Candidates

### 1. ZJK Industrial (NASDAQ: ZJK)
**What they supply:** Precision-machined metal components including liquid-cooling connectors (quick-disconnect couplings, cold-plate manifold fittings) for AI server racks. Won "2026 Yunfan Cup — Best Liquid Cooling Connector Supplier for Data Centers." Showcased QD connectors in NVIDIA MGX Ecosystem at COMPUTEX 2025.

**Size & forced-spend:** FY2025 (ended March 2026) revenue $56.1 M, +48% YoY; gross margin 43.7%, up sharply; net income $10.2 M, +177%. Market cap ~$200–300 M range. Zero sell-side coverage (Water Tower Research lone initiator, March 2026). US–Delaware JV with Chaince Digital (NASDAQ: CD) to build a US precision components gigafactory — supply-chain diversification tailwind. Small revenue base, and every CDU or cold-plate rack system needs dozens of QD fittings. If AI rack deployments double annually, QD demand pulls with it.

**Already ran? PARTIALLY — and then gave it back.** Stock hit a 52-week high >$30, then fell ~53% to ~$1.91–$2.47 as of early July 2026. The spike was partly hype-driven; the pullback creates a re-entry window IF financials confirm durable revenue growth. FY2025 results (released mid-2026) show genuine growth. Trades at ~26x trailing P/E vs. machinery-sector average ~29.5x.

**Key risk:** Chinese-headquartered (Shenzhen), Nasdaq-listed via IPO. Dual-class shares adopted March 2026. Classic VIE/China small-cap risks: governance, regulatory, RMB translation, US-China trade tensions on manufacturing localization. Flag: CHINA RISK HIGH.

**Accessibility:** Nasdaq-listed, US investor accessible. Flag China risk prominently.

### 2. Bel Fuse (NASDAQ: BELFB / BELFA)
**What they supply:** Power conversion products (AC/DC power supplies, DC/DC converters), magnetics (power transformers, power inductors, integrated connector modules). Specifically relevant: DC/DC converter bricks and inductors used inside server PSUs and in 48V-to-processor-rail conversion stages. Also acquired dataMate (Methode Electronics) March 2026 — adds high-reliability connector technology.

**Size & forced-spend:** FY2025 revenue $675.5 M, +26.3% YoY, record. Market cap ~$1.0–1.2 B. Book-to-bill 1.3 in Q4 2025, full-year bookings +75.8%, power segment bookings +172%. The 48V migration in AI servers is structural — every rack needs DC/DC conversion at multiple stages. Bel's power-conversion and magnetics product lines sit squarely in the power delivery chain.

**Already ran? NOT FULLY — lagging OEM-layer peers.** The stock has benefited from record results but trades well below the Vertiv-tier multiples. Wall Street has focused on aerospace/defense mix, under-appreciating the AI power buildout contribution. Bookings surge (+172% in power) is a leading indicator of revenue acceleration into 2026. Q1 2026 guidance $165–180 M implies annualized run-rate ~$680–720 M.

**Key risks:** Magnetics segment manufactured largely in China — RMB wage inflation and FX are margin headwinds (Q4 magnetics gross margin slipped to 27.3%). Customer concentration thin (no single customer >10%). Moderate leverage after $90 M debt paydown in 2025 ($197.5 M remaining). Diversified end-markets (aerospace/defense, industrial, networking) means AI is an accelerant but not the only driver.

**Accessibility:** US-listed, fully accessible. Manufacturing China exposure but equity is US domiciled. Flag China manufacturing risk.

### 3. Taiyo Yuden (TSE: 6976 / OTC: TAYOF)
**What they supply:** Third-largest MLCC maker globally (low-teens market share); also power inductors. AI servers use 20,000+ MLCCs vs. 600–800 in a smartphone — a ~25-35x per-unit content jump. Taiyo Yuden commercialized world's first board-embedded MLCC at 22 µF (1005 size) and 100 µF (2012 size) — both targeted specifically at GPU power delivery placement adjacent to die.

**Size & forced-spend:** FY2025 (ended March 2026) consolidated sales ¥355 B (~$2.3 B), operating profit +91% YoY, net profit +535% YoY. Book-to-bill 1.25 in Q1 FY2026 (Jan-Mar 2026), with quarterly orders hitting ¥111 B — first time above ¥100 B in ~5 years. Capacity expansion: +5% FY2025, +10% FY2026. JPMorgan projects operating profit trajectory: ¥22 B → ¥38 B → ¥74 B → ¥110 B (FY2025–2028).

**Already ran? YES — meaningfully re-rated.** Stock +175% over trailing 12 months, hit 26-year high. JPMorgan has a ¥22,500 target (aggressive), consensus at ¥4,422 — extraordinary analyst divergence. At elevated multiples the easy re-rating is done; further upside requires the MLCC tightness cycle to extend 2–3 more years (possible but not certain). Included here as the "already-ran" benchmark for the MLCC theme.

**Key risks:** Still-low profitability vs. peers (ROE 4.5%); Murata has stronger positioning in the highest-value GPU-adjacent MLCCs. FY2026 guidance missed consensus at release. Forex (JPY). Stretched P/S.

**Accessibility:** TSE-listed; accessible via OTC (TAYOF) or brokerage with Japan access. No China production risk.

### 4. Vishay Intertechnology (NYSE: VSH)
**What they supply:** Broad passives (MLCCs, resistors, inductors, capacitors, diodes, MOSFETs). For data centers: MLCCs and film capacitors for power filtering across PSU and VRM stages, as well as MOSFETs used in power switching. "Vishay 3.0" strategy targets $5 B revenue at 30%+ gross margin by 2028–2029.

**Size & forced-spend:** Market cap ~$3.6 B. TTM revenue ~$3.4 B. Q2 2026 guidance $875–905 M. Goldman Sachs called out capacitor makers as a likely upcycle through 2030, citing 220% projected datacenter power growth. AI servers carry disproportionate capacitor content; tightness in high-cap-density, low-voltage MLCCs is already emerging.

**Already ran? NOT OBVIOUSLY — consensus cautious, stock weak.** Stock around ~$20–27, 12-month consensus target ~$34, but one platform forecasts downside to $18. Recent June 2026 equity offering (15 M shares + $750 M notes) creates dilution/leverage overhang. Company has underperformed vs. AI-narrative peers. The "Vishay 3.0" story is not yet believed.

**Key risks:** Dilutive capital raise just completed June 2026. Passive-component cyclicality — revenues can swing sharply. Murata, TDK, Yageo dominate premium MLCC; Vishay is stronger in resistors/MOSFETs/film caps. Analyst consensus cautious.

**Accessibility:** US-listed, fully accessible. Diversified manufacturing (not China-concentrated).

### 5. Vicor (NASDAQ: VICR) — FLAG: ALREADY RAN
**What they supply:** High-density power conversion modules (vertical power delivery / VPD), 800V-to-48V factorized converters, Gen 5 VPD products for AI GPU racks. Directly addresses the hardest problem in AI power delivery: delivering 10,000+ amps at 0.7V to GPU packages with minimum loop inductance.

**Already ran — include as benchmark only.** +145% YTD as of mid-2026. Trailing P/E ~90x. Q2 2026 revenue guidance revised up. ITC IP investigation against infringing competitors adds royalty revenue tailwind. Analyst targets $217–$325 with stock at ~$336 — price already above most targets. IP monetization risk if ITC action fails or settlement terms disappoint.

**Do not initiate** at current prices without meaningful pullback. Mentioned here as the canonical "power module" play — if VICR fades, watch for valuation spillover to Bel Fuse and adjacent power names.

## Coverage Gaps

The following areas were identified but not fully diligenced within the 7-search budget:

1. **Dielectric / immersion coolants (pure-play):** 3M Novec successor products are now sold via Solstice Advanced Materials (NASDAQ: SOLS) — but SOLS is ~$3.9 B revenue (large-cap, spun from Honeywell Oct 2025), not small-cap. The pure-play immersion coolant angle has no confirmed small-cap public name; private companies (Engineered Fluids, GreenCool) dominate.

2. **CDU pump suppliers:** Most precision pump makers for liquid cooling (Grundfos, KSB, IDEX) are either private or large-cap diversified industrials. No pure-play small-cap CDU pump name was surfaced.

3. **Thermal interface material (TIM) pure-play:** Indium Corporation (relevant AI GPU TIM work), Laird (DuPont division), Bergquist (Henkel division) — none are standalone public small caps. This gap remains open.

4. **High-current busbar / DC distribution specialists:** Eaton and ABB dominate. No sub-$1 B market-cap public busbar pure-play was identified.

5. **Specialty connectors (beyond ZJK):** CEJN (QD couplings, private/Swedish), Amphenol (large-cap), Molex (private/Koch). The blind-mate QD connector space for liquid cooling is largely private. Worth a dedicated Japan/Taiwan small-cap search — Yamaichi Electronics, I-PEX, or similar.

6. **Japan MLCC second tier:** Murata (large-cap, already tracked) and Taiyo Yuden (run) dominate. A search for Walsin Technology (Taiwan), Yageo sub-entities, or Eyang (Taiwan) was not run — worth a targeted Taiwan small-cap MLCC screen.

7. **Solid-state transformer / GaN power conversion small caps:** GaN Systems (acquired by Infineon), Navitas Semiconductor (NVTS, ~$500 M mkt cap) was not analyzed — worth reviewing as a potential Slice 6 candidate given 48V/GaN adoption curve in AI rack PSUs.
