# Initial Research Findings

*2026-07-05. Synthesis of three parallel research sweeps: (A) world-model funding landscape, (B) VPG root-cause, (C) supply-chain map + already-moved names. Sources are in the underlying agent logs; key URLs cited inline.*

## Bottom line
The thesis is **half-right in a way that matters**. The capital wave is real and new (Assumption 1 ✅). But the flagship evidence — VPG as a "world model supply chain" beneficiary — **does not hold up (Assumption 3 ❌ for VPG specifically)**, and the causal mechanism (world-model *labs* → supplier revenue) is the **weakest link** in the chain. There is, however, a stronger *sibling* thesis sitting right next to it that the evidence actively supports. The job of diligence is to decide which thesis we're actually underwriting.

---

## Finding 1 — The capital wave is real, heavy, and front-loaded (✅ supports Assumption 1)
- World-model category funding jumped ~5x YoY: ~€1.3B (2024) → ~€6.5B (2025); Bessemer estimates ~$6B into 6–7 world-model companies in **Q1 2026 alone**.
- Marquee rounds cluster tightly in **Nov 2025 → Jun 2026**: Luma ($900M, 2-GW cluster), Physical Intelligence ($600M→$5.6B, then $1B/$11B talks), Skild ($1.4B/$14B), Runway ($315M/$5.3B), Wayve ($1.2B+/$8.6B), World Labs ($1B, Autodesk $200M anchor), AMI Labs / Yann LeCun ($1.03B seed, Paris), Decart ($300M), Odyssey ($310M/$1.45B).
- Nvidia (NVentures) is a repeat backer *and* chip vendor *and* model builder (Cosmos 3) — a flywheel. Not a locked ecosystem: Odyssey dropped Nvidia for AMD/AWS Trainium; AMD Ventures is on many cap tables.
- **Read-through:** fresh billions raised explicitly for compute, robot fleets, and data infrastructure; capital typically converts to POs over 2–4 quarters → a physical-inputs capex bulge into **H2 2026 – H1 2027**. Directionally supports the timing premise.

## Finding 2 — The VPG "world model" attribution is UNSUPPORTED (❌ for the flagship)
The stock move is real (roughly +140–180% over 3–4 months; ~+330–430% over 12 months; ATH $151.78 on Jun 30). But the driver is **not** world models:
- The catalyst was the **Q1 FY2026 print (May 12)**: orders $102.1M (first >$100M since 2022), book-to-bill 1.21, backlog $125M, plus a new 3-yr margin-expansion model + Russell index inclusion. Reward was for the **order/backlog inflection**, not profits (it was a small GAAP loss).
- Management's *own* attribution of the order surge, in descending real dollars: **(1) precision resistors for semiconductor test equipment + AI data centers + fiber optics; (2) defense / avionics / military / space; (3) industrial weighing (incl. precision ag).**
- **Humanoid robotics = ~$600K shipped in Q1 (<1% of ~$330M revenue); ~$5M cumulative prototype orders over ~2 years.** A real but tiny call option the market is pricing aggressively.
- **"World model" appears in zero VPG calls/filings.** Management's frame is "Physical AI" = embodied robots sensing the real world (force/torque/strain). That is the *actuation/sensing layer of robots* — unrelated to world models (the ML technique of an agent simulating its environment).
- Valuation: trailing P/E ~270x vs. 5-yr median ~20x; average analyst target (~$95) sits *below* the current price. Momentum/narrative re-rate far ahead of realized fundamentals.

**Verdict:** VPG is an AI-*hardware*-infrastructure + defense story with a humanoid-robotics call option. Attaching it to "world models" is a narrative graft. This is the single most important correction to the thesis.

## Finding 3 — The already-exploded names are driven by humanoid robotics + the compute/memory supercycle, NOT world-model labs
Names that already ran in early 2026 and their real drivers:
- **Memory/HBM (ran first + hardest):** Micron +~210% YTD, SK Hynix +~248%, Samsung +~165% — structural HBM shortage, "supercycle." Driven by hyperscaler AI compute, not world models.
- **Lidar/perception:** Ouster +~150–189%, Aeva +~81% — "physical AI" perception narrative.
- **Test / robotics-adjacent:** Teradyne +~139% (70% of Q1 rev AI-tied), Vertiv (cooling/power, backlog +109%, orders +252%).
- The trigger for the humanoid supply chain specifically was reportedly the **Agility Robotics IPO** (SPAC), not lab spending.
- **Pattern the market rewards:** confirmed order/revenue inflection + a clean narrative label. Adjacency alone doesn't move stocks; a booked-orders catalyst does.

## Finding 4 — The mechanism problem (the crux the thesis has to survive)
Two demand vectors are being conflated, and only one is the thesis as literally stated:
- **World-model software labs** (World Labs, AMI, Physical Intelligence, Runway, Cosmos) spend overwhelmingly on **GPUs + talent**. Their explicit strategy — **synthetic data generated in simulation** — *reduces* their need for physical real-world data-collection hardware. GPU/compute dollars flow to **mega-caps** (Nvidia, hyperscalers, memory makers) where they *cannot* multiply a small supplier's revenue.
- **Physical AI / humanoid robot builders** (Agility, Figure, Unitree, etc.) consume the **physical BOM**: force/torque sensors, actuators/reducers, cameras, lidar, IMUs. This is where the small-cap "revenue multiplication" math can actually work — but the demand driver is **robot unit deployments**, not world-model labs.

**Implication:** the specific chain "world-model *labs* spend → small supplier revenue multiplies" is the weakest formulation. The dollars that scale with lab spend go to mega-caps; the small-cap supplier upside is tied to *robot deployment volume*, which is a related-but-different clock.

---

## The fork this creates
- **Thesis A (as literally stated):** world-model *labs* are the demand engine. Evidence so far: weak — spend concentrates in mega-cap compute; VPG attribution failed.
- **Thesis B (the sibling the evidence supports):** the **physical-AI / humanoid + compute-infrastructure buildout** is the demand engine; world-model labs are one contributor among several (labs, robot OEMs, hyperscalers, defense). Evidence: strong and already partly played out.

Thesis B is more likely true but also **more crowded** (many names already ran). The alpha question becomes: within Thesis B's supply chain, which nodes have NOT re-rated AND have a credible revenue-multiplication path AND a watchable order signal? That is a sharper, more defensible version of the original hunt.

## Sizing / "dwarf" reality check
The robotic-sensor market itself is modest (~$0.83B in 2026 → ~$1.24B by 2031, ~8% CAGR). For revenue *multiplication* to occur at a supplier, the incremental order flow must be large vs. that supplier's *current* base — which is plausible only for **small-caps with a concentrated design-win into a high-volume robot/data-center program.** This is the exact screen the scanners must encode.
