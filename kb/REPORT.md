## The HSAI downgrade, in plain terms

Last fire (89), this campaign's #1 name for 15+ fires — Seyond Holdings — was removed after a cold re-derivation found its "AV thesis exposure" was actually ordinary passenger-car driver-assist demand from NIO, not humanoid/robotaxi demand. That same fire flagged an explicit follow-up: HSAI's own robotaxi-fleet claims (Zoox, Mercedes-Benz L3, Motional, 8-of-top-10 robotaxi developers) had never had the same rigorous decomposition — they'd just been taken at face value.

This fire ran that decomposition. Hesai's own FY2025 SEC filing (6-K) already draws the exact line needed: it separates "ADAS" (ordinary passenger and commercial vehicles) from "Robotics" (robotaxi fleets, humanoid/quadruped robots, robovans, lawn mowers, AGVs) as two distinct reported segments. ADAS is 85.2% of unit volume; Robotics is 14.8% — and within that small Robotics slice, robotaxi+humanoid is further diluted by lawn-mowers and delivery robots. Critically, **Mercedes-Benz L3 — a program this KB had been counting as "genuinely robotaxi/L3+-grade" — sits in Hesai's own ADAS bucket.** It's a passenger-vehicle consumer feature, not a qualifying buyer.

Once decomposed honestly: genuinely-qualifying robotaxi+humanoid revenue (real, primary-sourced buyers: Zoox, Motional, Pony.ai, WeRide, Baidu Apollo Go, DiDi, Unitree, HONOR Robot, Galbot, Magiclab, Vita Dynamics) is an estimated single-digit-to-low-teens percent of Hesai's total revenue — not the ~90%+ the carried 7/8 score implicitly assumed. An Opus re-derivation scores this honestly at ≈5/8 (Gate 1 not-re-rated 0.5/2 — cheap but a fully-discovered $2.35B mega-name at 5x the ideal size ceiling; Gate 2 named buyer 1.5/2 — the best qualifying roster in the KB, but it drives only ~10% of revenue; Gate 3 multiplies/capture-rate 1/2 — RoboSense, the #1 global robotics-lidar vendor, is out-executing Hesai's own diversification story in exactly this sub-segment; Gate 4 financial health 2/2 — GAAP-profitable, ~$1B net cash, not the crux).

**Unlike Seyond, HSAI is not being fully removed.** It keeps a real, primary-source-named qualifying-buyer roster and clean financials — that earns it a low-conviction monitor slot rather than a strict kill. A stricter reading of TARGET-PROFILE.md (market cap >$500M, thesis exposure <15%, founded >10yr ago, an ADAS-dominated revenue base) would fully justify removal; the orchestrator's call this fire is to hold one notch short given the genuine qualifying core.
