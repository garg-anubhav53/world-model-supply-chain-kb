## The Seyond downgrade, in plain terms

For 15+ fires, Seyond Holdings (2665.HK, Chinese lidar maker) held the #1 spot at a 7/8 score. Every individual fact checked out: NIO is genuinely a named, disclosed, growing customer; shipments genuinely are exploding (+385% YoY); the stock genuinely is cheap (at an all-time low). What never got checked, explicitly, was whether NIO's lidar demand is actually *inside* this campaign's thesis. NIO buys lidar for ordinary consumer passenger cars with driver-assist features (L2/L3 ADAS) — not for robotaxi fleets, not for L4+ autonomous driving, not for humanoid robots. That's a different, much larger, more mature market than "humanoid/AV/world-model," even though the sensor hardware looks similar. Once the revenue is honestly decomposed: genuine humanoid/AV-robotaxi exposure is ≤12.3% (and even that slice isn't confirmed humanoid), and there is no named buyer from this campaign's actual target universe in any Seyond filing anywhere.

This was sitting in the KB's own prior dossier entry (Fire 86: "thesis-exposure ~95-100% automotive AV/ADAS... humanoid/non-auto ~0-5%") for a full fire cycle before anyone ran it through the campaign's own 2-question quick-kill. **This is not a claim that Seyond is a bad stock or a troubled business** — it may well be a perfectly reasonable automotive-lidar investment. It simply does not fit what this specific campaign screens for, and the accumulated shortlist inertia (15+ "confirm, don't re-derive" fires) let a category-boundary error persist long after the disqualifying fact was on record.

**Full reasoning:** companies/SEYOND.md (Fire 89 section), decisions-log.md, pipeline.md. **Re-open tripwire:** a named humanoid/AV-robotaxi/world-model buyer + non-automotive revenue share credibly >20-25% at the Aug-31 2026 H1 interim (or later).

**Process fix applied this fire:** TARGET-PROFILE.md amended with an explicit "What counts as AV" section distinguishing robotaxi/L4+/humanoid demand from ordinary passenger-vehicle ADAS. trends.md T24 records the generalizable lesson: a named buyer + disclosed relationship + real revenue growth is not sufficient if the buyer's own end-market is outside the thesis definition — Gate 2 needs a third question, not just "is there a named buyer."
