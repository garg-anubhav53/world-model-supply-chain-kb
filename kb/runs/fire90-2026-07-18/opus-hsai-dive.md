## 1. The crux — exposure decomposition (this now DOMINATES the court/EGM catalysts)

### The category error the prior 7/8 embedded
Every prior fire's Gate-2/Gate-3 credit ("8-of-top-10 robotaxi," "NVIDIA DRIVE Hyperion 10," "Mercedes-Benz L3," "40 OEMs / 160 models," "3-3.5M unit guide," "10M+ backlog") counted Hesai's **entire AV/ADAS book** as thesis exposure. Per Hesai's OWN FY2025 6-K segment definition (primary source, verbatim in the scout note), that book is split:

- **"ADAS" = "passenger and commercial vehicles"** — Li Auto, Xiaomi, Changan, Mercedes-Benz L3, Great Wall, Toyota, GAC, BAIC, FAW, etc. **85.2% of FY2025 units (1,381,133 / 1,620,406).** This is ordinary consumer/commercial driver-assist. Under the campaign's post-Seyond rule ("What counts as AV," TARGET-PROFILE.md line 47-48) it is **100% NON-QUALIFYING** — the same bucket as Seyond's NIO/Onvo/SAIC-VW/GAC book that triggered the Fire-89 downgrade.
- **"Robotics" = robotaxi + humanoid + robovans + lawn-mowers + AGVs** — **14.8% of FY2025 units (239,273).** Only a *sub-fraction* of THIS bucket qualifies (robotaxi + humanoid); the rest (lawn-mowers, AGVs, delivery robovans) does not.

**Mercedes-Benz L3 is the smoking gun.** It has been cited as "robotaxi/L3+-grade AV exposure" across Fires 8/13/34/40/59/80/81/87. It is a **consumer-owned passenger-car feature** (DRIVE PILOT-style conditional automation, "10+ China JV models / 1M+ units," individually purchased vehicles) — squarely in Hesai's OWN "ADAS" bucket. This is the identical error the Seyond downgrade caught. It does NOT qualify.

### Sizing the genuinely-qualifying (robotaxi + humanoid) revenue
Hesai discloses **no** revenue-dollar or %-split by application (confirmed: FY2025 20-F, Q4/FY2025 release, Q1 2026 call — units only). So this is an explicit estimate, shown:

- FY2025 total revenue RMB3,027.6M (~$433M); 1,620,406 units → blended ASP ~RMB1,868. Blended GM 41.8%, with Hesai stating ADAS carries **lower** GM than Robotics → Robotics is higher-ASP than its 14.8% unit share implies. Reasonable **Robotics revenue share ≈ 15-30% of total** (~$65-130M).
- WITHIN Robotics, the qualifying part (robotaxi + humanoid) competes for share against high-volume/low-ASP robotic lawn-mowers (the "300k-unit deal," "10M+ backlog" are lawn-mower/consumer-robotics volume, NOT humanoid/robotaxi) and robovans/AGVs. Robotaxi is high-ASP/low-volume (4× AT128/vehicle, ~RMB4k/unit); humanoid is nascent (Unitree JT128, lower ASP).
- Bottom-up robotaxi: ~61% robotaxi lidar share, on the order of ~10k fleet vehicles × 4 lidars × ~RMB4k ≈ RMB160M, + Zoox $40M+ order (~RMB145M/yr amortized) → robotaxi ~RMB200-300M. Humanoid ~RMB20-50M.
- **Qualifying (robotaxi + humanoid) ≈ RMB250-350M ≈ $35-50M ≈ ~8-12% of total revenue.** This corroborates the scout's "single-digit-to-low-teens %."

**Conclusion: ~85-90% of Hesai's revenue is non-qualifying passenger/commercial ADAS. The genuinely-qualifying robotaxi+humanoid slice is ~8-12% of revenue — a small, diluted sub-segment of a company whose growth story is driven by mainstream ADAS.** This is structurally the Seyond problem. The *difference* from Seyond: Hesai's qualifying slice is REAL and primary-source-named (Seyond's was zero).

## 2. Four-gate re-derivation (8-point rubric), thesis-exposure corrected

### Gate 1 — Not-yet-re-rated / quality-adjusted valuation: **0.5 / 2**
- Price $15.00 (2-source, stockanalysis.com + Google Finance, 2026-07-17). **ADS count 156.44M** (8x trap avoided — do NOT use 1.257B ordinary-share count). **True mkt cap = 156.44M × $15.00 = $2.35B** (~4-5x TTM sales, GAAP-profitable).
- TARGET-PROFILE operational rule: flag founding year + thesis-exposure BEFORE price. Hesai founded **Oct 2014 → ~11.75 yr (>10, unfavorable)**; thesis-exposure ~8-12% (**<15%, unfavorable**). Both unfavorable → the profile's own "SKIP" line applies: *"Any company older than 10 years where thesis-market exposure is <15% — even if it's cheap, the stock will be driven by its 20 other businesses."* HSAI is a textbook match for that SKIP.
- Quality Score = (thesis-exposure % × causal-lock-in × visibility) / (EV/rev vs comps). With exposure corrected from ~100% to ~10%, the numerator collapses. It's cheap (near 52-wk low, −34% YTD) and profitable, but it's a **fully-discovered $2.35B, 21-Strong-Buy consensus mega-name** ($2.35B is ~5× the profile's <$500M ceiling — a robotaxi/humanoid ramp is a footnote here, not a re-rating). Half a point for genuine cheapness + beaten-down price; no more, because the discount is regulatory, not thesis-obscurity, and the thesis exposure is thin.

### Gate 2 — Airtight causal link / named buyer (in primary filings): **1.5 / 2**
- **This is HSAI's strongest, and it SURVIVES the decomposition.** Genuinely-qualifying buyers named in Hesai's OWN primary 6-K filings:
  - **Robotaxi (fleet/L4):** Zoox ($40M+ order, delivery through end-2026, Amazon-owned), Motional (exclusive short-range on IONIQ 5), Pony.ai (4× AT128/vehicle, 7th-gen), WeRide, Baidu Apollo Go, DiDi.
  - **Humanoid/embodied-AI:** Unitree (JT128 in **all** its humanoid robots), Galbot, Magiclab, Vita Dynamics, HONOR Robot.
  - This is the **single best qualifying-buyer roster of any name in the KB** — far stronger than Seyond ever had (Seyond had zero genuine qualifying buyers).
- **Deductions (−0.5):** (a) these buyers drive only ~10% of revenue — the *causal link that actually moves the company* is passenger-ADAS, not these names; (b) **Aurora** flagged UNVERIFIED (moving to in-house "FirstLight" lidar, mid-2026 — likely a lost/at-risk qualifying customer); (c) NVIDIA DRIVE Hyperion 10 is a mixed platform channel (spans L2/L3 consumer + L4), not a clean qualifying buyer; (d) robovans (Zelos/Neolix/Meituan) are gray-zone L4-delivery, not passenger-robotaxi/humanoid.

### Gate 3 — Revenue multiplies / capture-rate (of the QUALIFYING segment): **1 / 2**
- **For the segment itself:** yes, it can multiply in % terms — robotaxi is inflecting (Zoox commercial, Baidu/Pony/WeRide scaling) and humanoid is nascent-but-real. Off a ~$35-50M base.
- **For the THESIS (does qualifying revenue multiply enough to drive the STOCK):** no. Even a 3-5× of the qualifying slice by FY2027-28 (~$150-250M) reaches only ~20-25% of a ~$650-800M total — it can't re-rate a $2.35B company where 75-90% of value is ADAS + regulatory optionality. And you cannot buy the segment standalone.
- **Capture-rate:** strong on robotaxi (sole/preferred on Pony, Motional exclusive short-range, Baidu, ~61% robotaxi lidar share) but on a small absolute base; **contested on the fastest-growing qualifying sub-segment** — **RoboSense is #1 globally in robotics lidar (282,600 units H1 2026, +510% YoY), out-shipping Hesai in exactly the robotics/humanoid space** and is itself 1260H-listed. So: good capture, small base, real competition where it matters most. One point.

### Gate 4 — Financially healthy: **2 / 2** (not the crux)
- GAAP-profitable (FY2025 net income RMB435.9M; 4+ consecutive profitable quarters; Q1 2026 +29.6% YoY, GM 39.1%), ~$1.0B net cash, positive operating cash flow 3 straight years, not a VIE. Clean. This is real and is the one gate the decomposition doesn't touch — but it's also why HSAI is NOT a KILL on fundamentals.

### **TOTAL: ~5 / 8** (down from the inflated 7/8). The 2-point drop is entirely the exposure correction landing on Gates 1 and 3 — the same mechanism as the Seyond re-derivation.

## 4. Capture-rate assessment (explicit)
- **Robotaxi: strong but small.** Sole/main lidar on Pony.ai (4× AT128), exclusive short-range on Motional IONIQ 5, main supplier Baidu Apollo Go, Zoox $40M+ (likely 1-of-N as Zoox may multi-source), ~61% robotaxi lidar share (down from ~74% historically). Genuine preferred-supplier lock-in on a base that is still only thousands of fleet vehicles.
- **Humanoid: real design win, contested field.** Unitree "all humanoid robots" (JT128) is a strong single-account position, plus Galbot/Magiclab/Vita/HONOR — but 1-of-N across the humanoid field, and **RoboSense leads robotics lidar globally and is growing +510% YoY**, out-executing Hesai in the exact qualifying sub-segment.
- **Net:** best-in-KB *breadth* of qualifying named buyers, credible preferred-supplier economics on robotaxi, but (a) small absolute base, (b) the fastest-growing qualifying sub-segment (robotics/humanoid) is where the strongest competitor is winning, (c) one qualifying customer (Aurora) appears to be in-sourcing.

## 6. Why DOWNGRADE and not the other three verdicts
- **Not PARKED-unchanged:** the 7/8 was inflated by the passenger-ADAS category error; honest re-derivation is ~5/8. The "armed-to-promote-to-#1" thesis (Fire 86: "moves to #1 above Seyond on a favorable ruling") is invalid — it was premised on treating the ADAS book as thesis exposure. Leaving it PARKED-armed would repeat the exact mistake the campaign just corrected on Seyond.
- **Not PROMOTE-as-standalone-thesis:** the qualifying segment (~$35-50M rev, ~$0.2-0.7B pure-play value in BEAR/BASE) is too small and too diluted inside a $2.35B ADAS company to drive a re-rating, and it is not separately investable. It only "matters to the multiple" in the BULL (3-5× ramp), which is 2-3 years out and competition-contested. Optionality, not a promotable thesis.
- **Not (quite) KILL/REMOVE — though defensible:** a strict TARGET-PROFILE reading says REMOVE, identical to Seyond (>$500M cap, <15% exposure, >10yr, ADAS-driven). I stop one notch short of full REMOVE because — unlike Seyond, whose "AV exposure" was 100% passenger-ADAS with ZERO genuine qualifying buyers — **HSAI has a real, primary-source-named robotaxi + humanoid core (the best qualifying-buyer roster in the KB) and is GAAP-profitable with $1B net cash.** That optionality is worth a low-conviction monitor slot rather than deletion. **DOWNGRADE = strip the inflated score/rank, reclassify as monitor-only; if the orchestrator wants campaign purity, REMOVE is the fully-defensible stricter call.**

## 7. What flips the verdict (either direction)
**Toward RE-PROMOTE / upgrade (would need to break the exposure ceiling):**
- Hesai begins **disclosing robotaxi/robotics revenue separately** and it is materially higher than estimated (>20-25% of revenue) AND growing >100%/yr.
- A **disclosed 8-figure+ humanoid contract** (Unitree/Figure-scale) that makes embodied-AI a named revenue line, not a design-win footnote.
- **Robotaxi fleet volume inflection** (Baidu/Pony/Zoox to tens of thousands of vehicles) lifting qualifying revenue past ~20% of total.

**Toward KILL / full REMOVE:**
- Confirmation qualifying revenue is <10% and flat/shrinking (e.g., Aurora-style in-sourcing spreads; Zoox/Motional multi-source away).
- RoboSense continues taking robotics/humanoid share (already #1, +510% YoY).
- Adverse 25-5256 ruling amputating the US robotaxi book (the highest-quality qualifying names are US fleets).

**Note:** a favorable court ruling or clean EGM would flip the STOCK bullish but would NOT flip this thesis-fit verdict — HSAI stays an 85-90% ADAS company either way.
