## LESSON THIS FIRE — two new patterns, both about not trusting a single data point at face value
(1) **When two price checks disagree, check the DATE of each source before assuming one is stale.** One dive read Novanta's pre-selloff Jul-2/6 price ($158-165) as "more current" than the correct, independently-reconfirmed post-selloff Jul-7 price ($147.85) — the two figures were sequential, not conflicting; a real intervening move (the already-logged sector-wide semicap contagion) made both readings correct for their own moment. (2) **A customs shipment record alone is not sufficient corroboration for a supply relationship.** Avride's own materials state its delivery robots are manufactured in Taiwan; the customs record pointed to a Kunshan-mainland-China shipper — a logistics/consolidation node can masquerade as a final-assembly site. Cross-check the buyer's own stated manufacturing geography before promoting a customs-only lead.
