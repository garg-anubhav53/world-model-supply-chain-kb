## Verdict
**WATCH (materiality-capped)** — not the clean KILL the bench-promotion note anticipated, but not a strong BUY/conviction name either. Novanta is a rare case of a diversified industrial company with *genuinely disclosed* physical-AI/robotics exposure (a real Amazon warehouse win, an NVIDIA ecosystem partnership, real ATI force/torque hardware, active humanoid-OEM engagements) — the force-torque thematic fit that justified the original bench-promotion is real, not invented. But it fails this KB's core edge on **materiality and pricing**: a ~$1B two-segment company already trading near multi-year highs at ~5.5x sales, where robotics/physical-AI cannot plausibly multiply *total* company revenue several-fold even in a bullish scenario. Stays WATCH; would only escalate toward BUY conviction if (a) a named humanoid OEM discloses quantified volume/content-per-unit terms AND (b) the multiple compresses from a broader de-rate.

## Adversarial bear case
The entire robotics bull case rests on a TAM slide ("$1B opportunity by 2030" is Novanta's own end-market estimate, not booked revenue) and one concrete disclosed win — $50M over three years, ~1.5%/yr of revenue, with an *unnamed* buyer. Humanoid exposure is pre-revenue ("we're talking to everybody"). Meanwhile organic revenue actually *fell* 0.5% in FY2025 and AET grew only ~2% last quarter, while the stock trades near all-time highs at a growth-company multiple (~5.5x sales, ~22x EBITDA) for what is fundamentally a GDP-plus medical/industrial compounder. Every product category is contested by entrenched competitors with no evidence of an exclusive lock. If physical-AI capex timing slips to "'27-'28" as management itself frames it, the multiple could de-rate well before any incremental revenue shows up — the March 2026 dip to $118 (from prior highs) already previewed how sensitive the stock is to sentiment on this exact narrative.
