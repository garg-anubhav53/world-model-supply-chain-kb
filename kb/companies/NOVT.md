## Fire 33 — first full gate-check
This name sat on the KB's WATCH shortlist for 14+ research cycles on a purely thematic basis (force-torque sensing corner, filling Precision Optics' vacancy after its Fire 32 KILL) with **no individual dossier or diligence ever performed**. This is that first pass, done from scratch.

**Snapshot:** HQ Bedford, MA. Two reportable segments only — **Automation Enabling Technologies** ($500.8M FY2025: precision motion, encoders, servo drives, ATI force/torque sensors, photonics/lasers) and **Medical Solutions** ($479.8M FY2025). "Robotics & Automation" is a product-line/capability inside AET, not a broken-out disclosed number. FY2025 total revenue **$980.6M** (+3.3% y/y, organic -0.5%) — corrects a prior KB-file presumption of ">$2B revenue," which was wrong by ~2x.

**Market cap (2-source):** ~$5.58B (stockanalysis.com, $147.85, 2026-07-07) / ~$5.76B (Yahoo/Macrotrends, $161.72, 2026-07-02). Note volatility: an Investing.com snapshot dated 2026-03-16 showed $118/~$4.2B — the stock has moved meaningfully within the year. Financials pristine: cash $380.9M, total debt $250.8M → net cash +$121M; FY2026 guide $1,030-1,050M rev / $245-250M adj. EBITDA (~24% margin), raised.

**Evidence for a named forced-buyer (Gate 2 core) — stronger than most bench names, but no fully-named OEM:**
- **"World's largest e-commerce player"** (Amazon, unnamed in the disclosure) — warehouse-automation content win, **$50M revenue opportunity over 3 years**, bookings taken, revenue starting 2026, "crescendo in '27-'28" (Q3 2025 earnings call).
- **10+ humanoid engagements** — company describes "working with pretty much everybody… leading players" in NA/Europe, but **zero named** humanoid customers or unit volumes.
- **NVIDIA Halos AI Systems Inspection Lab** partner (Mar 2026) — validates Novanta motion/sensing hardware for warehouse/industrial/humanoid use cases (incl. IGX Thor) — a certification signal, not a revenue disclosure.
- **Real force-torque product line**, not aspirational: Axia80 Kit, Omega191 (from the acquired ATI Industrial Automation, a genuine robotics F/T leader), plus Everest servo drives, Omni+ frameless motors, IncOder encoders. This vindicates the bench-promotion's thematic instinct — the force-torque corner IS a real Novanta product line — but none of it is disclosed as sole/exclusive to a specific humanoid program.
- Management's own TAM framing: a "~$1B end-market opportunity for Novanta by 2030" from combined warehouse+humanoid physical-AI — an addressable-market claim, not booked revenue.

## Gate scores (0-2 each)
1. **NOT re-rated — 1/2.** Up ~38% over the trailing year, near 52-week highs ($98-$172 range), trading ~5.5x sales / ~22x EBITDA; FY2026 guidance was explicitly raised on "AI, medical, automation." Not a pure humanoid blow-off (this is a diversified medtech/industrial compounder), but the robotics/physical-AI narrative is already partially priced into the multiple.
2. **Causal link — 1/2.** A quasi-named Amazon $50M design win + NVIDIA Halos partnership + 10+ humanoid engagements + real ATI force/torque SKUs clears the bar of "more than a narrative" — but every component category (drives, encoders, motors, force/torque sensors) is contested by Kollmorgen, Harmonic Drive Systems, Maxon, TE Connectivity. No fully-named humanoid OEM, no disclosed unit-volume/content-per-robot figure, no evidence of sole-source lock-in.
3. **Revenue MULTIPLIES / materiality — 1/2, the binding drag.** Physical-AI/robotics-forced revenue is a minority sliver of a ~$1B diversified base today (estimated well under 15%; the $50M/3yr Amazon win alone is ~$15-17M/yr, ~1.5-1.7% of revenue). AET itself grew only ~2% in the most recent quarter checked. Even a strong multi-year robotics ramp inside AET is unlikely to multiply Novanta's *total* revenue several-fold this decade — this trips the KB's standing "total revenue >$800M + exposure <30%" materiality kill-condition, though the underlying segment growth is real and not zero.
4. **Financial health — 2/2.** Net cash positive, ~24% EBITDA margins, strong FCF generation, no distress signals whatsoever.

**Total: 5/8.**

## Verdict
**WATCH (materiality-capped)** — not the clean KILL the bench-promotion note anticipated, but not a strong BUY/conviction name either. Novanta is a rare case of a diversified industrial company with *genuinely disclosed* physical-AI/robotics exposure (a real Amazon warehouse win, an NVIDIA ecosystem partnership, real ATI force/torque hardware, active humanoid-OEM engagements) — the force-torque thematic fit that justified the original bench-promotion is real, not invented. But it fails this KB's core edge on **materiality and pricing**: a ~$1B two-segment company already trading near multi-year highs at ~5.5x sales, where robotics/physical-AI cannot plausibly multiply *total* company revenue several-fold even in a bullish scenario. Stays WATCH; would only escalate toward BUY conviction if (a) a named humanoid OEM discloses quantified volume/content-per-unit terms AND (b) the multiple compresses from a broader de-rate.

## Adversarial bear case
The entire robotics bull case rests on a TAM slide ("$1B opportunity by 2030" is Novanta's own end-market estimate, not booked revenue) and one concrete disclosed win — $50M over three years, ~1.5%/yr of revenue, with an *unnamed* buyer. Humanoid exposure is pre-revenue ("we're talking to everybody"). Meanwhile organic revenue actually *fell* 0.5% in FY2025 and AET grew only ~2% last quarter, while the stock trades near all-time highs at a growth-company multiple (~5.5x sales, ~22x EBITDA) for what is fundamentally a GDP-plus medical/industrial compounder. Every product category is contested by entrenched competitors with no evidence of an exclusive lock. If physical-AI capex timing slips to "'27-'28" as management itself frames it, the multiple could de-rate well before any incremental revenue shows up — the March 2026 dip to $118 (from prior highs) already previewed how sensitive the stock is to sentiment on this exact narrative.
