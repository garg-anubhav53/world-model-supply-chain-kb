# Jiangsu Azure Corporation / 蔚蓝锂芯 (formerly 澳洋顺昌) — SZSE: 002245.SZ

**Verdict: KILL** (found-and-Opus-gate-checked Fire 23, 2026-07-07). Genuine primary named-buyer disclosure exists — rare in this KB — but the stock has already re-rated as a "robot-concept" mid-cap and the actual robotics revenue is an immaterial rounding error against a $1.1B legacy base.

## Identity (verify-before-diligence lesson applied)
Ticker 002245.SZ listed Shenzhen SME board since Jun 2008 as 江苏澳洋顺昌 (Jiangsu Aucksun/Ausunny) — renamed the ENTIRE listed parent to **江苏蔚蓝锂芯 (Jiangsu Azure)** in Dec 2020. "蔚蓝锂芯" is the current legal brand of the listed entity itself, not a hidden subsidiary — no "Cooler Master"-style misidentification risk here. Battery operations sit in subsidiary 江苏天鹏电源 (Jiangsu Tianpeng); a legacy LED-chip/sapphire-wafer segment sits in 澳洋顺昌科技材料. This is a diversified holding co: cylindrical Li-battery (power-tool cells, ~12% global share, #3 behind Samsung/EVE) + LED chips + IT-distribution/logistics.

## The claimed thesis
Unitree Robotics (宇树科技) as a named customer for humanoid-battery cells: ~60% share of Unitree's 2024 cell sourcing, a custom 18650 cell, "exclusive" H1 energy-module supply, 2025 volume guided to ~600,000 units, joint 350Wh/kg semi-solid battery development.

## Why it's KILLED (Opus adversarial gate-check, Fire 23)
1. **Gate 1 (not re-rated): FAIL — binding gate.** Price ¥19.02 (Jul 1 2026, 2-source), market cap ~¥21.9B (~$3.05B), 12-month range ¥11.45–28.50 — up ~66% off the low to +149% at the peak, explicitly described in Chinese press as "蹭上宇树科技成机器人概念股" (rode Unitree to become a robot-concept stock), promoted since Feb/Mar 2025. P/E ~31x vs. historical ~15-20x. Already-run.
2. **Gate 2 (causal link): PARTIAL (1/2).** A genuine primary disclosure exists — 投资者互动平台 (互动易), Mar 21 2025: "宇树科技是公司锂电池业务客户" (Unitree is a lithium-battery customer), the company's own words. But that is ALL the company confirmed; the 60% share, "exclusive H1," and precise unit-volume claims are media/promotional elaboration (Eastmoney caifuhao, Stockstar, OFweek), not company disclosure. Unitree is explicitly one of three named robot buyers (alongside UBTech/优必选 and Zhiyuan-AgiBot/智元) — no sole-supplier moat.
3. **Gate 3 (revenue multiplies): FAIL.** The "600,000 units" figure is a CELL count, not a robot count (media conflation) — at ~¥8/cell disclosed value, 600,000 cells ≈ ¥4.8M of robotics-specific revenue, ~0.06% of FY2025's ¥8.11B total revenue. Even a 10x scenario is <1%. Company itself concedes "真正上量还需几年" (real volume is years away). The segment's real bulk remains legacy power-tool cylindrical cells.
4. **Gate 4 (financial health): PASS (2/2).** Genuinely healthy — FY2025 revenue ¥8.11B (+20.1%), net profit ¥711M (+45.66%) — but this health is driven entirely by the legacy power-tool-battery + LED businesses, not the humanoid thesis.

**Total revenue ($1.13B) also trips the size-gate** (>$800M with <30% — here <<1% — exposure).

## Bear case (survives)
A diversified ~$3B mid-cap whose shares were bid up ~1.5-2.5x as a retail robot-concept proxy on the back of one 互动易 sentence. Real robot exposure is ~¥5M/yr. Moat is weak (1-of-3 named suppliers, commoditizing 18650 cell, both EVE/CATL and Unitree itself have every incentive to insource cell sourcing). Valuation has already priced the optionality.

## Process lesson (for the KB, not just this name)
A genuine primary-disclosure named buyer is necessary but not sufficient. Always check MATERIALITY (content-$ × volume vs. total revenue base) and the PRICE gate before getting excited about disclosure quality — this is the cleanest "real buyer, immaterial + already-priced" case the KB has found, worth remembering as its own failure archetype distinct from "no named buyer at all."
