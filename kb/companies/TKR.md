# The Timken Company — TKR (NYSE) [Spinea]
Updated 2026-07-09 (Fire 43, first-ever genuine full 4-gate re-derivation) | Verdict: **KILL** (score 2/8)

**Context — 3rd instance of the "undiligenced legacy placeholder" pattern (after ON Semiconductor/Fire 41 and Klingelnberg/Fire 42):** TKR sat "PARK, score 2, SCALING, no change" for many fires with only price-check reconfirms, never a genuine 4-gate re-derivation. It was carried in the KILL-LIST mega-cap "ALREADY KILLED" row as one of only two "genuinely-live" legacy names (with NOVT). This re-derivation resolves it. The KB conflated "TKR (Spinea) = cycloidal reducer" but never scored the actual parent company — which is a $9.6B diversified industrial-bearings mega-cap, exactly the wrong profile for this KB's small-base-multiplier shortlist.

**What:** Timken is a ~$4.6B-revenue diversified engineered-bearings + industrial-motion company (19,000 employees, 44 countries). It owns Spinea (cycloidal reducers/actuators, Prešov, Slovakia — acquired 31 May 2022 for $151.2M, ~$40M annual sales), alongside Cone Drive (harmonic/strain-wave + precision gearing, 2018), Rollon (linear motion), and CGI (precision miniaturized gears, 2024). These form its "robotics & automation" product cluster, now reported inside the **Industrial Motion** segment ($1,563.7M FY2025, ~34% of sales; Engineered Bearings is the other $3,018.1M, ~66%).

- **Gate 1 (not re-rated): FAIL 0/2 — AUTOMATIC DISQUALIFIER (same failure axis as ON Semi/Fire 41).** Stock ~$139-142 (Yahoo Finance/Robinhood, 2026-07-09), hit **all-time high $142.94** intraday (Investing.com, ~2026-06-30) and new 52wk high $145.72 (MarketBeat, 2026-06-30). **+102.1% 12-month** (Simply Wall St), **+65.4% YTD 2026**, +23.4% past month. 52wk range **$70.57-$146.37** — trading at the very TOP, essentially doubled off the low. Mkt cap ~$9.6B (~69.5M shares). Driver of the run is a broad industrial re-rating: Q1 2026 EPS beat ($1.67 vs $1.50), FY2026 guidance raised to $4.70-5.20 (later $5.50-6.00 range cited), JPMorgan upgrade to Overweight, PT $150, new-CEO (Lucian Boldea) turnaround narrative — a large-cap industrials story, NOT a hidden small-cap. Decisively already-run, 2+ independent sources.
- **Gate 2 (causal link airtight): FAIL 0/2 (same failure mode that killed Klingelnberg/Fire 42).** No named humanoid-robot OEM anywhere in primary sources. The **Investor Day 2026 deck** (s27.q4cdn.com, 2026-05-20): zero mentions of Tesla Optimus, Figure AI, Unitree, UBTECH, Agility, Apptronik, 1X, Boston Dynamics — and no robotics/humanoid revenue dollar or % breakout. Q1 2026 call confirms "active participation in humanoid and exoskeleton markets" and double-digit automation CAGR since 2018 — thematic/aspirational, no design-win, no PO, no named buyer. This is precisely the "components are used in robot joints generally" narrative with zero named buyer that killed Klingelnberg.
- **Gate 3 (multiplies): FAIL 0/2 — MATERIALITY TRAP (same structural pattern as ON Semi & Klingelnberg, and NOVT/Fire 33).** Spinea was ~$40M of sales at acquisition — <1% of Timken's $4,581.8M FY2025 revenue. Even the ENTIRE robotics/automation cluster (Spinea + Cone Drive + Rollon + CGI) sits inside a $1.56B segment dominated by conventional industrial motion, and humanoid-specific volume within that is a rounding error. No plausible humanoid scenario multiplies a $4.6B, $9.6B-cap base: a generous $50-150M humanoid ramp over years is 1-3% of revenue — noise. Structurally too large and diversified to ever matter, identical to why ON ($6B rev/$38.7B cap) and KLIN (€285M rev) were killed.

No red flags — a well-run, investment-grade industrial. Financial health is genuinely strong; it simply has nothing to do with the humanoid thesis.

- **Score: 2/8 (first-ever explicit 4-gate score).** Fails Gate 1 AND Gate 2 AND Gate 3 — a worse gate-count than ON Semi (failed 1+3) or Klingelnberg (failed 2+3). The only passing gate (financial health) is thesis-irrelevant.

**Adversarial / bear case (the kill rationale):** TKR never belonged on a "not-yet-re-rated small-cap supplier" shortlist. It is a $9.6B diversified bearings mega-cap trading at an ALL-TIME HIGH after a 100%+ 12-month run driven by a cyclical industrial recovery + CEO-turnaround re-rating + raised guidance — the re-rating already happened, and not for humanoid reasons. Its humanoid exposure (Spinea cycloidal + Cone Drive harmonic) is real hardware but (a) has no disclosed named humanoid customer, and (b) is <1% of revenue, so even a home-run design-win is immaterial. It is the SUPPLIER-SIDE mirror of the same trap that killed ON and KLIN: real thematic adjacency, zero named buyer, zero multiplication.

**Pure-play alternative check (task item 5):** No sharper listed pure-play cycloidal-reducer name emerges that isn't already dispositioned. Nabtesco (TYO:6268) is the dominant global RV/cycloidal-reducer maker but is itself a ~$2.5-3B-revenue diversified group (already in seed-map, industrial not humanoid-named). The China cycloidal/reducer complex the KB already worked is exhausted: Shuanghuan/双环传动 (002472.SZ — real Unitree/Optimus reducer, but $5.6B cap + near 52wk high, KILLED), Zhejiang Fine-Motion/环动科技 (largest customer Estun *industrial* robots, KILLED), Leaderdrive/绿的谐波 (harmonic, already-run). **No spin-off/carve-out rumor exists** — Timken management explicitly frames Cone Drive/Spinea/Rollon as a cluster to *integrate and scale*, not divest (multiple 2026 sources). So there is no cleaner way to "own Spinea" than owning all of Timken, which strictly dilutes the thesis.

**Verdict: KILL** for the humanoid-robotics supply-chain thesis. Removed from active PARK/pipeline. If tracked at all, belongs on a large-cap "industrial-automation incumbents" watchlist, not this KB's small-base-multiplier shortlist. Do NOT re-open without a *dated, named* humanoid-OEM design-win disclosed in a Timken filing/press release AND a robotics/humanoid revenue breakout large enough to matter against a $4.6B base — neither of which has ever existed.
