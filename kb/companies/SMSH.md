# Smart Shooter Ltd — SMSH (TASE)
Updated 2026-07-07 (Fire 12) | Verdict: **WATCH (new, promoted from PENDING)** | Track: **defense/drone-autonomy (adjacent vertical)**

- **What:** Israeli maker of the SMASH fire-control/target-acquisition system (hardware trigger-inhibit — weapon fires only when the barrel is physically aligned to hit). IPO'd on the Tel Aviv Stock Exchange March 8, 2026 (~NIS 900M valuation, ~$240M).
- **Price (2-source, Jul 7 2026):** ~2,524-2,600 ILS (investing.com, stockanalysis.com), mkt cap ~NIS 1.33B (~$383-385M), P/E ~77. IPO low was 1,650 ILS (Mar 30 2026), ATH 2,819 ILS (Jun 3 2026). **Up ~50% vs IPO valuation and ~58% off the March low in four months** — much of the asymmetry is already priced; this is a soft-kill on entry timing even though it's too new for a standard 12m read.
- **Gate 1 — NOT re-rated:** SOFT-FAIL. Genuinely too new for a 12m read, but the rapid post-IPO re-rate (+50-58% in 4 months) means this isn't a fresh, undiscovered story anymore.
- **Gate 2 — Causal link:** STRONG. Real, current, multi-branch/multi-nation named-buyer contracts, not marketing: US Army ~$10.7M follow-on (May 11 2026, via PAE Defensive Fires/ADS, delivery Q3 2026); USMC ~$3.4M+options to $5.8M (Q4 2026 delivery); US Navy ~$1.8M (Jun 2026); JIATF-401 ~$2.4M (Mar 2026); UK MoD £4.6M (225 SMASH sights to VHR units, fielded via Viking Arms, British Army selected SMASH for the SA80 A3 in 2023). Combat incumbency: in IDF service as "Dagger" since 2018.
- **Gate 3 — Revenue multiplication:** PASSES. $36.8M FY2025 revenue (+50% YoY), $39.65M order backlog with short delivery windows (Q3/Q4 2026) — plausible to convert within 24 months. ~$55M 2026 revenue guided (~+50% YoY, per one analyst source — not company-confirmed, treat as indicative).
- **Gate 4 — Financial health:** No distress flags found in this pass.
- **Capture-rate — the binding gate:** UNCONFIRMED/WEAK. No sole-source or program-of-record lock found — orders are distributor-routed (PAE/ADS in the US, Viking Arms in the UK), consistent with an incumbent COTS supplier, not a captured program. The Army's ~$108M FY squad/individual-soldier C-UAS budget line doesn't name SMASH or disclose vendor mix. Named competitors (Elbit ReDrone, Rafael Drone Dome, IAI Drone Guard) operate in adjacent/larger C-UAS categories, not the same handheld-sight niche — SMASH's moat is combat incumbency, not exclusivity.
- **Vertical-integration flag:** PARTIAL. US Army CCDC Armaments Center adapted "Gunslinger" fire control (from the FARA program) for ground-to-air C-UAS on CROWS remote weapon stations (tested Apr 2026, Aberdeen) — but this targets the vehicle-mounted/RWS niche, not the soldier-borne handheld SMASH 2000LE driving current orders. Not a direct hit yet.
- **Displacement risk:** MODERATE. Software-only AI target-recognition on existing optics is the long-term theoretical threat, but SMASH's hardware trigger-inhibit requires physical integration into the firing mechanism, raising the displacement bar versus a pure vision-app.
- **Adversarial bear case:** Valuation already rich (P/E ~77, ~10x TTM sales, ~7x even on optimistic forward revenue) — the easy re-rate has happened. Not a captured supplier (distributor-routed COTS, no program-of-record). Individual contracts are small/lumpy ($1.8-10.7M), tender-by-tender, losable to a cheaper qualified vendor. Heavy customer/geography concentration (Israel MoD ~47% of 2024 revenue, still ~22% in 2025) tied to a defense-budget cycle that could normalize post-conflict. March 2026 IPO lock-ups (90-180 days) roll off through mid-to-late 2026, adding share supply into a stock that already doubled.
- **Verdict: WATCH.** Named-buyer and revenue-mechanism gates pass cleanly on real, multi-branch/multi-nation contracts — genuinely one of the stronger causal stories in the KB. But capture-rate is unconfirmed (COTS/distributor-routed, not sole-sourced) and the stock has already re-rated meaningfully since IPO. Flip to BUY: a formal US DoD/Army sole-source or IDIQ program-of-record naming Smart Shooter as the standard soldier-borne C-UAS fire control, ideally with a valuation pullback toward IPO levels. Flip to KILL: the Army's in-house Gunslinger (or a cheaper qualified rival) wins the soldier-borne niche, a lost recompete, or a lock-up-expiry break confirming the re-rate has topped.
