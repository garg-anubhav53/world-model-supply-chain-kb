# TrunkTech (主线科技/Zhuxian Keji, Beijing) — pre-IPO (HK Chapter-18C filer)
Created 2026-07-16 (Fire 76) | Verdict: **KILL — business-model-fit failure.**

## NAMING CORRECTION (important — fix at source)
Fire 75's DIGEST/pipeline entry named this company "中智行" (CIDI) — that is WRONG. TrunkTech's actual Chinese name is **主线科技 (Zhuxian Keji)**. 中智行/CIDI is a different, unrelated Chinese AV company. Do not conflate; if 中智行 is independently worth checking, treat it as a fresh, never-diligenced name, not a "same company, different alias" case.

## Fire 76 — full diligence, KILLED on business-model-fit
TrunkTech is a full-stack **AV solutions/systems-integrator**, not a hardware-component manufacturer. It builds a proprietary "vehicle-edge-cloud" L4 autonomous-driving software+hardware stack sold as end-to-end solutions across three use cases: Trunk Port (logistics-hub unmanned transport), Trunk Pilot (highway freight), Trunk City (urban transport). It installs its own driving system onto trucks supplied by OEMs (FAW Jiefang, Sinotruk, Sany Heavy Truck) — those OEMs are TrunkTech's **vehicle-platform suppliers**, not buyers of a TrunkTech component. Same business shape as Pony.ai/WeRide (AV solution provider), just for trucking. **This is the inverse of our target profile** (hardware component supplier with a named OEM buyer) — same fail-mode as any AV/robotics solutions or fleet-operator company, not a supply-chain hit regardless of who backs it.

No named OEM or fleet buyer is disclosed anywhere in IPO reporting — cited "customers" are port operators (Tianjin Port, Ningbo-Zhoushan Port, Zhengzhou Inland Port), which are deployment/service sites, not component buyers.

**Size (secondary, not the binding gate here):** no official valuation disclosed in the Chapter-18C filing. Revenue RMB 112M (2022) → RMB 254M (2024, ~$35M) → RMB 99M (H1 2025). Analyst comps benchmark against Pony.ai (33.6x P/S) / WeRide (21x P/S); even at the low end (WeRide's 21x on ~$35-50M revenue) implied market cap could reach **~$700M-$1.7B** — plausible cap-gate breach too, but moot given the business-model kill.

**Founding/backing:** founded March 2017. Bosch Ventures invested via a 2019 Series A+ round (also GLP). Autonomous trucking is its sole business — genuinely young/focused, which is exactly why the business-model distinction matters: a young, thesis-focused company can still fail the screen if what it sells is software/solutions rather than a physical component.

**IPO status:** filed HK Chapter-18C application 2025-12-11 (HKEX filing confirmed). No hearing date or listing date set as of 2026-07-16.

**Lesson for the target profile (added to TARGET-PROFILE.md this fire):** "Bosch Ventures portfolio company" or "named in a VC portfolio-mining sweep" is necessary but not sufficient — VCs that back the hardware supply chain (Bosch Ventures, Nvidia NVentures, etc.) also back AV solutions/software companies. Always confirm what the company actually SELLS (a physical unit into an OEM's BOM vs. a software/driving-system service) before running the 4-gate check, not after.
