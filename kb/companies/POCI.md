- **What:** Micro-cap (~$62M mkt cap) maker of miniature/stereo optics, endoscopes, endocouplers, micro medical cameras for minimally-invasive and robotic surgery. Found via Fire 10's medical-robotics adjacent-vertical force-open.
- **Price (2-source, Jul 2026):** $5.69 (investing.com) / $5.65 (StockTitan, Jun 10 2026). 52wk range $3.55-$6.04 — roughly flat over 12m.
- **Gate 1 — NOT re-rated:** PASS, clean. Well within 52wk range, no blow-off move.
- **Gate 2 — Causal link:** FAILS on "forced." Near-certain (not confirmed) buyer: **Procept BioRobotics (PRCT)** — product/timing fit is exact (Procept's HYDROS AI-powered BPH platform, FDA 510(k) Aug 21 2024, ships with a pre-assembled single-use digital cystoscope = POCI's disclosed "single-use endoscope imaging assembly"; POCI's own Oct 29 2024 PR describes an unnamed "surgical robotics company in urology" whose AI-powered BPH platform just cleared FDA). Neither company has named the other. **Critical finding: the licensing agreement explicitly permits the buyer to insource** — POCI's CEO confirmed on the Q3 FY2026 call that the customer is *already* funding a duplicate production line at its own facility, with POCI degrading to a royalty on units it doesn't build. This is the opposite of a captive/forced-buyer relationship.
- **Gate 3 — Revenue multiplication:** WEAKER THAN HEADLINE. FY2026 guide raised to $29-31M (from $26-28M, ~52-62% growth off FY2025's $19.1M) — but Q3 FY2026 mix shows the robotics/cystoscopy line is only **25% of revenue ($2.2M of $8.7M)** and its per-unit economics erode as volume shifts to the customer's own line. The ACTUAL growth driver is an unrelated **sole-source aerospace program (41% of revenue, $3.6M)** — which the customer just asked POCI to slow 15-20% in Q1-Q2 FY2027. Intuitive Surgical (the original "named buyer #1") is no longer a material line item.
- **Gate 4 — Financial health:** CAUTION. Cash was $0.9M at 12/31/25 (near cash-out) before a **March 2026 dilutive raise** (2,777,777 shares @ $3.60, ~$9.3M net, priced below the then-~$4.49 market) brought cash to $10.7M. Q3 FY2026 Adj-EBITDA was +$0.3M (first positive quarter) but full-year FY2026 is still guided to a loss of $2.5-2.7M; gross margin only 23.6%; bank debt ~$1.5M (not large). Approaching breakeven, but a dilution rescue, not a fortress balance sheet.
- **Capture-rate — the binding gate:** UNFAVORABLE. Non-exclusive by contract on the robotics program (customer can and is insourcing); POCI's one genuine sole-source relationship is on the unrelated aerospace program, not robotics.
- **Adversarial bear case:** the "forced buyer" is not captive — it's actively insourcing right now (Procept is a ~$2B, ~64%-gross-margin razor-blade-model company with every incentive to own its disposable COGS). Program concentration is severe for a $30M company (top 2 programs = 66% of revenue), and the bigger one (aerospace) is pulling back. The robotics line is a shrinking-economics ~25% slice. Balance sheet needed a below-market raise months ago. Tiny-team execution risk (yield targeting 95% only by Q4).
- **Verdict: WATCH (weak), leaning KILL on the specific robotics-forced-buyer thesis.** Improving fundamentals (record revenue, first positive quarterly EBITDA, un-re-rated stock) make this an interesting general micro-cap turnaround, but it fails gate 2's "forced/captive" requirement outright — the buyer is demonstrably insourcing. Not promoted to the conviction shortlist. Revisit only if: the Procept relationship is confirmed exclusive/expands, a NEW captive robotics program is disclosed, or the insourcing reverses.
