(2) **NOT sole/preferred — fails the capture-rate test cleanly.** HERE Technologies (TomTom's main HD-map rival) is owned by a consortium that includes **Audi** — a Volkswagen Group brand — meaning VW Group has a direct ownership stake in TomTom's own competitor, and HERE independently supplies mapping content to Volkswagen among other OEMs. This is a clean 1-of-N vendor situation within the very same OEM family, not a forced/exclusive position. (3) **The relevant segment is shrinking, not multiplying:** Location Technology segment revenue €114M in Q1 2026 (-6% YoY); Automotive sub-segment €76M (-5%) / €70M operational (-16% YoY), explicitly attributed by management to "gradual discontinuation of certain customer programs." FY2026 guidance for the whole segment is €435-485M vs €482M in 2025 — flat-to-down, not growth. No other named AV/robotics customer exists beyond CARIAD; management only gestures vaguely at "a healthy pipeline in 2026, 2027" with no names. **Verdict: KILL** — undisclosed/unverifiable deal value + confirmed non-exclusive vendor position (HERE/Audi-linked) + a shrinking, not multiplying, segment together fail the causal-rigor checklist on 2 of 3 legs. Re-open trigger: a disclosed CARIAD contract value that clears the size test AND evidence of sole/preferred status vs. HERE specifically.
