## Where we stand (honest)
This was a "resolve, don't discover" fire. Ten sourcing scouts covered the usual wide set of vectors — Israel geography, China A-share, Chinese trade press, ImportYeti customs, EDGAR, Taiwan/Korea/Japan, buyer-prospectus mining, forward-listing catalysts — and every one of them came back with either a dead end, a status update, or (twice) a scout-reported "new candidate" that turned out to already be on the kill list. That's a legitimate outcome, not a failure: the KB's own standing lesson is that a dry sourcing fire should still move the conviction track forward, and this one did, on all three of the fire's open binding gates.

**Intops Co Ltd (KOSDAQ: 049070)** got its materiality gate — open since it was promoted last fire off an unusually clean ImportYeti customs record — resolved with an actual number, which is rarer than it sounds. Intops doesn't disclose a robotics-EMS revenue line, but a sell-side analyst's bottom-up build (units shipped to Bear Robotics per year, times price) puts it at roughly 4-5% of total revenue. That's real — well above the sub-1% materiality traps that have killed a dozen-plus China A-share names in this KB — but it's also been flat at that level for five years, and the "wearable robot manufacturing platform" pivot the company talks about is still pre-revenue. What's genuinely new and worth tracking: Bear Robotics is now 61%-owned by LG Electronics, which just stood up a CEO-level robotics business unit and called 2026 a "foundation year" — a credible reason the flat line could finally move, with the obvious risk that LG could just as easily vertically integrate around Intops instead of scaling through it. Stays WATCH; the question for Q2 earnings (~August) is simply whether unit volume actually moves off 5,000/year.

**Woorim (KOSDAQ: 101170)** needed more correcting than confirming this fire. Three things were wrong in how it entered the KB last fire: the name itself ("P&ES" should be "PTS," Power Train Solution), the sourcing (it is *not* actually named in the Korea Herald factory-tour article that was cited as putting it alongside Hyundai Mobis, Hwashin Precision, and SOS Lab — that's a genuine conflation of two separate stories), and the business description (excavator transmissions and industrial reducers, not aerospace gearboxes). What survives the correction is a real, on-record quote from a Woorim company official to a Korean financial outlet back in November, affirming an ongoing (pre-mass-production) reducer relationship with Boston Dynamics. That's a genuine step up from anonymous press aggregation — the company itself said it, on the record, and hasn't walked it back — but it's still short of a filed disclosure or any confirmation from Boston Dynamics or Hyundai. Price action stays clean (near its 52-week low, genuinely not re-rated). Stays WATCH-weak, with a sharper binding trigger now: a DART filing, a counterparty naming, or a disclosed mass-production order, or this gets killed next fire.

**Seyond Holdings (2665.HK)** got its first genuine staleness-guard re-derivation since being promoted to WATCH at Fire 43 — three fires of light price-checks in between is exactly the pattern this KB's own rules say should trigger a full re-look. The re-derivation found real movement in both directions. On the bull side: NIO's mass-market sub-brand, Onvo, started actually attaching LiDAR in Q2, which is a genuinely new demand leg beyond the original NIO-flagship story, and gross margin turned positive for the first time. On the bear side: Hesai sued Seyond for patent infringement in October (still unresolved), and Huawei has surged into the #2 spot in China's ADAS-LiDAR market, ahead of RoboSense on the narrowest recent cut — a new and serious competitive threat neither Hesai nor Seyond had to contend with a year ago. Revenue itself actually fell slightly in FY2025 despite unit volume up 45%, because average selling prices dropped a third in an intensifying price war — so the "revenue multiplies" gate is still more promise than proof. Net effect: the 4-gate score moves up a point, 5 to 6 of 8, but the move is closer to a wash than a real re-rating case — cheaper, more clearly a real forced-buyer story, but also now carrying a live litigation overhang and a tougher #2. Also worth flagging for the KB's own records: this is the *second* time a WebSearch-summarized TradingView price for this specific ticker has come back wrong by roughly 3.7x — a direct source (stockinvest.us or Simply Wall St) should be used for Seyond from now on, not TradingView.
