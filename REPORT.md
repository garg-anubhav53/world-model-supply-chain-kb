## Where we stand (honest)
No promotions and no WATCH-tier kills this fire — the drought that Fire 37 briefly broke is technically back, but the substance of this fire is not stagnation. The headline decision is Luyuan Group Holdings, the China e-bike maker carried PENDING since Fire 37 on an unverified cooperation deal with Youlu ("Deer") Robotics for reducer joint modules. This fire's verification found the harder question — is Youlu a legitimate forced-buyer at scale? — genuinely resolves nuanced: Youlu is real, funded (>RMB100M round backed by Yuanjing Capital, Sinovation Ventures, Baidu Ventures), and revenue-generating (~RMB600M cumulative orders by March 2025), founded by Alibaba DAMO Academy's former robotics-lab head. But its actual shipping products are wheeled/mobile-form retrofits — cleaning vehicles, forklifts — not bipedal humanoids; its own roadmap lists "人形机器人" (humanoid) as a future stage, not current production. None of that ended up mattering, though: Luyuan's own market cap ($600-800M, confirmed 2-source) decisively exceeds the $300M widened-bar WATCH cap on its own. The lesson worth keeping: check the cheaper gate first. A size-gate check can moot a much harder causal-legitimacy question in one pass.

The second event is a genuine methodological upgrade, not just a status update. SOS Lab, the Korean LiDAR maker that has anchored this KB's shortlist since early fires, got its second-ever genuine staleness-guard full re-derivation (the first was five fires ago, at Fire 33). The fresh from-scratch work reconfirmed the bear case rather than softening it: Q1 2026 revenue came in at ₩0.58B, down both year-over-year and sequentially from Q4 2025's ₩2.53B — the exact quarter this KB had previously flagged as evidence of a "full-scale supply ramp" beginning. Financial health remains genuinely clean (~₩47B liquid, 11.4x paid-in capital, no impairment, roughly two years of runway), so this isn't a going-concern kill. But rather than repeat the now-familiar "stays WATCH(weak), recheck later" verdict, this fire sets an explicit, dated commitment: the H1 2026 semiannual report, due around mid-August, is the keep/kill decision point. If it shows no ramp, the next fire downgrades to KILL — full stop, not another extension. This is a sharper version of the precedent set by Momentus and Airgain's own staleness-guard re-derivations, and it should make SOS Lab's eventual resolution (whichever way it goes) unambiguous rather than another multi-fire drift.

The third event is negative but valuable: two separate sourcing sub-threads confirmed genuinely exhausted this fire, closing off ambiguity rather than leaving it open.
