TKR — the Timken Company, which owns the cycloidal-reducer maker Spinea — got its first-ever genuine full 4-gate re-derivation after sitting on this pipeline as "PARK, no change" for dozens of fires, and it was KILLED. NOVT is now the only genuinely-live name left in the KB's legacy mega-cap row.

The second, more interesting decision is a downgrade that corrects the KB's own past work rather than just re-confirming it. Xinjian Transmission was promoted to WATCH back in Fire 37 on the strength of what the KB described as a confirmed named buyer — Tesla itself, with a 1,000-unit-plus mass-production order for dexterous-hand assemblies. A genuine staleness-guard re-derivation this fire, six fires after that promotion with nothing but light status checks in between, found both headline claims were overstated. The actual ChiNext prospectus doesn't name Tesla at all — it uses an anonymized "Customer A," and "Tesla" is press/market inference layered on top of that. And the 1,000-unit order traces only to unsourced market rumor; a named Tier-2 supplier (Demax) has publicly denied cooperating on it, and the actual disclosed figure in Xinjian's own primary source is 109 assemblies — about a ninth the size. The real underlying business (dual-source, not sole, into Tesla's roller-screw supply chain; ¥31M of robotics revenue growing fast off a tiny base) survives, so this isn't a kill — but it's been demoted out of the top-5 conviction shortlist to a lowest-priority, track-only position, with a pre-committed automatic KILL trigger the moment it prices (which will almost certainly be well over the size cap) or the moment the exchange's inquiry round forces a real answer on who Customer A actually is.

The third decision runs the other direction. Seyond Holdings, the distant-#3 Chinese LiDAR maker with a clean sole-source relationship to NIO, has sat on-deck for roughly thirteen fires blocked by a $300M market-cap line it never quite cleared. This fire, the size logic itself got re-examined rather than just the price — and the KB's own actual practice elsewhere (how it kills names like Dongfang Electric Heating and Hi-Target, both explicitly "over the $800M hard cap") makes clear that $800M, not $300M, is the real WATCH-eligibility ceiling; $300M is just the preferred sweet spot. Seyond's ~$483M market cap, sitting at its 52-week low, clears that bar cleanly. It's promoted to WATCH — but logged weak, not a BUY-track name, because its financial health is genuinely bad (a $328M net loss, 7.9% gross margin, still a distant #3 by shipment volume, and unresolved litigation from Hesai).

Beyond those three decisions: an India geography sweep (round 7 of this dimension) came back genuinely negative but cleared out three long-carried mystery triggers — most notably discovering that "REPM," which the KB had internally assumed was some kind of robotics procurement tender, is actually India's Rare Earth Permanent Magnet manufacturing scheme, unrelated to humanoids. And a data-integrity issue resurfaced: an AI-search-summarized stock price for Seyond came back flatly wrong (a fabricated market cap nearly 4x the real figure, with a made-up "all-time low" to match) — caught and corrected against two direct sources, but a reminder not to trust single-source AI-summarized prices.
