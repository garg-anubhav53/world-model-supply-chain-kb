## Where we stand (honest)
The headline event this fire is a KILL, not an addition — and it's the single most consequential housekeeping decision in many fires. ON Semiconductor (ON) had sat on this pipeline as "WATCH, no change" for more than 40 fires, carried forward purely as an undiligenced legacy placeholder — the KB's own notes had flagged it as a likely artifact worth investigating, but no one had ever actually run it through the 4-gate process from scratch. This fire did. The result is unambiguous: ON fails Gate 1 decisively (up +61.2% over 12 months by price and +74.2% by market cap, two independent sources, +137% year-to-date, already doubled off its 52-week low) and fails Gate 3 on a structural materiality trap identical in shape to what killed NOVT's bull case down to a WATCH in Fire 33 — ON is a $38.7B-market-cap, $6B-revenue diversified semiconductor company where image sensors are roughly 16% of revenue, mostly automotive ADAS, and actually losing CMOS-sensor share to Sony. No named humanoid-robot buyer exists anywhere in ON's disclosure record; every robotics reference found is generic marketing language, never a design-win or a named customer. This is exactly the failure mode the KB's staleness-guard rule exists to catch: a name doesn't stay stable because nothing has happened, it stays "unchanged" because no one is looking. ON scores 3/8 — its first-ever explicit score, assigned on the way to a kill. Klingelnberg (KLIN.SW) got a lighter companion check on the same theme and comes out the other way: still genuinely un-re-rated (~CHF 91M market cap, trading near its 52-week low), so it legitimately stays WATCH unchanged.

The second theme this fire is a caught dedup failure — worth reporting honestly because it's a real process gap, not a success story dressed up. The China A-share sourcing scout was handed a curated inline excerpt of the KILL-LIST (roughly 50 names, not the full ~280-line file) as an efficiency measure, per this KB's own standing instruction to pass dedup context directly into scout prompts. It still re-surfaced five already-killed names as apparent "near-miss" candidates: Zhongding Sealing Parts (killed Fire 37, size gate), Jiangsu Azure/蔚蓝锂芯 (killed Fire 23), Hanyu Group/汉宇集团 (killed Fire 35), Bukom/步科股份 (already on the kill list), and Haozhi Mechatronics/昊志机电 (killed Fire 37). All five were caught before publication by cross-checking the complete KILL-LIST.md at the synthesis stage, rather than trusting the scout's own dedup pass as final — but the near-miss shows that a curated excerpt is not a reliable substitute for a full-file check, and that lesson is now logged for future fires.

The third theme is a clean negative from the customs/ImportYeti rotation (ring cursor=1): five genuinely new 2025/2026-vintage merchant AV and delivery-robot entrants were tried (Vay, Verne/Rimac, Glydways, DeepRoute.ai, Cyngn), and all five were dead ends — no US customs footprint, a private/immaterial supplier, or (in Verne's case) a third instance of the "homonym false-positive" pattern this KB has now seen with Aurora Innovation and Avride/Coretronic: ImportYeti's "Verne" record resolves to an unrelated Chilean company, not Rimac Group's actual robotaxi.
