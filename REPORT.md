**Huayi Technology (华依科技, 688071.SH) is this fire's one new WATCH — and it's a good example of why the KB runs many independent scouts.** Three separate scouts, working three different angles (a broadened positioning/timing-node search, a Chinese trade-press sweep, and a China A-share 互动易 sweep), independently converged on the same company: an automotive powertrain test-equipment maker whose IMU line — a diversification, not its core business — supplies attitude sensors to Zhiyuan/AgiBot humanoid robots. That's a real, company-sourced, dated disclosure naming a real buyer, on a $400-420M market cap that hasn't re-rated (+4.06%/12m). The honest complication: FY2025 swung to a net loss (-¥61.65M), and 2 of the 3 scouts that found this name flagged it for an outright kill on that basis alone. We carried it to WATCH(weak) instead, on the same logic that kept Intops on the shortlist through 5 consecutive loss quarters — a real causal story with a genuine financial question mark isn't the same as a going-concern problem, and no scout found distress language. If losses widen further at the next quarterly report, this converts to a kill.

**Longxi Bearing (龙溪股份, 600592.SH) is a new PENDING, not WATCH — and it's a useful case study in why disclosure quality alone doesn't override a company's own words.** This one has an unusually strong relationship on paper: an 85%-owned joint venture that is Zhiyuan's exclusive joint-bearing supplier for the 远征A2 humanoid, plus a formal "Excellent Partner Award" from Zhiyuan. That's better evidentiary quality than most China A-share finds in this KB, which are typically a vague cooperation MOU or a trade-show showcase. But Longxi's own risk disclosure states the humanoid business is still in R&D/small-batch sample-testing with "extremely limited near-term contribution" — nearly identical language to what killed Shuangyi Technology last fire. We logged it PENDING rather than a hard kill, because the relationship itself is real and well-documented; it just isn't revenue yet.

**Intops' recurring unexplained price pops got a dedicated investigation and came back reassuring but inconclusive.** Three consecutive ~7% intraday pops with no matching DART filing. The explanation: a hot-then-correcting Korean robotics theme (LG Electronics quadrupled then halved in 2026) plus a genuine broadening of Intops' own story — it's no longer just "the Bear Robotics supplier," it now has disclosed relationships with Samsung's "Bot Fit" wearable (mass-production standby) and Hyundai/Kia's "X-ble Shoulder" wearable (already in mass production, KS-certified). ## Standing lessons this fire
(1) **A node that keeps testing negative under its "obvious" company universe is worth re-approaching with a broader frame.** Positioning/timing tested negative 3 times under a strict GNSS/RTK-pure-play definition; broadening to "any small company with a named buyer in this general sensing category" found Huayi Technology on the first try. (2) **Independent convergent discovery is a real, if mild, confidence signal.** Three scouts, three different methods, one company — that's stronger corroboration that the underlying disclosure is genuine than any single scout's report, even though the final promote/kill call still needs the orchestrator's own judgment on the other gates. (3) **A company's own admission of near-term immateriality caps a name at PENDING regardless of how good the disclosure quality is otherwise.** Longxi Bearing's JV-plus-award evidence is better than most of this KB's China finds, but "extremely limited near-term contribution" in the company's own risk factors is the same shape that's killed other names — the right call is PENDING with a specific recheck trigger, not a promotion on disclosure quality alone, and not a permanent kill either.
