## Where we stand (honest)
Thesis INTACT, no net pipeline change this fire, but the news is mixed rather than a clean stall. The headline event is AIRG's first-ever GENUINE staleness-guard full re-derivation — every prior fire's "check" on Airgain had been a light re-read of the same March/May 2026 filing, never a real from-scratch pull. This fire finally did the real work and found the KB had been anchored on a slightly wrong story: the Coco Robotics design win was first disclosed 2026-03-12, not the May 6 8-K, and after 4 full months there is still zero dollar figure or purchase order — just a repeated "multi-million-dollar opportunity over the life of the rollout." Doing the multiplication arithmetic properly for the first time (2 low-cost cellular modems per robot × a plausible 10,000-unit fleet target) puts the realistic lifetime value at $2-4M against a ~$50M diversified three-segment revenue base — a bump, not a multiplier. That's a decisive Gate-3 fail. This is NOT a kill, though: Coco Robotics itself is genuinely healthy and scaling ($80M Series B, 500K+ deliveries, Sam Altman-backed), and Airgain's own balance sheet is debt-free and inflecting toward breakeven independent of robotics. So AIRG stays WATCH but drops out of the top-5 conviction shortlist, with a freshly-confirmed hard test now on the calendar: the Q2 2026 earnings call, dated Aug 7 (release) / Aug 11 (call) 2026 — if that call still discloses no numbers on Coco or the still-unnamed second autonomous-VTOL customer, next fire should treat this as a live KILL candidate.

The second notable event is a genuinely fresh sourcing find that still resolved to a kill. A round-6 sweep of Israel geography (this fire's rotation-cursor focus) surfaced Foresight Autonomous Holdings (FRSX) — a real, small ($34M), heavily de-rated (-87%/2025) AV-sensing company with a genuine named-buyer disclosure: VisionWave Holdings (Nasdaq: VWAV) signed a definitive agreement to integrate Foresight's sensors into its VARAN autonomous ground platform. On the surface this clears the size and price gates cleanly. But VisionWave is ALSO acquiring up to 52% of Foresight in the same transaction — meaning the "buyer" is becoming the controlling shareholder. This is the same structural problem this KB has already seen twice in China (Fenglong Co, where UBTECH was both controlling shareholder and buyer; Aishida, similar framing) — future "sales" from Foresight to VisionWave would be effectively intercompany, not arm's-length forced spend. Confirming this pattern recurs outside China is itself a useful finding: it's now a standing screen to apply to every new candidate, not just China A-share names.

Elsewhere, sourcing continues to show diminishing returns: a fresh AV sensor-pod BOM teardown method (validated on Waymo, Fire 30) was retried on three new buyers — Zoox, Mobileye, Aurora — and came back negative on all three, either fully vertically integrated or routing disclosed supply through mega-cap/already-tracked names (Teledyne FLIR, Hesai, STMicro, OmniVision). A China A-share/HK fresh sweep (dedup-first) found 12 dead-ends, all resolving to already-killed tickers or gate-fails — Unitree's now-effective IPO registration is generating a wave of trade-press re-coverage of names this KB has already assessed, not new ones. The one piece of genuine forward progress outside the shortlist: Xinjian Transmission progressed from listing counseling to a formal SZSE ChiNext "accepted" status, with disclosed robotics-segment revenue growth of +346% YoY.
