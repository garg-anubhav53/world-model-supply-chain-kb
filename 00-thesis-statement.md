# Thesis Statement — World Model Supply Chain

*Recorded 2026-07-05. This is the thesis as originally articulated, preserved before any pressure-testing.*

## Core claim
A large, recent wave of venture + strategic capital has flowed into "world model" companies (World Labs / Fei-Fei Li, Yann LeCun's startup, and peers). That capital is heavy and new enough that these companies will **spend aggressively over the next 6–12 months** on parts of their supply chain — regardless of whether world-model *capability* actually pans out. The spend is somewhat mechanical: freshly-raised money converts to purchase orders.

## The trade
Vishay Precision Group (VPG) is cited as a supplier that has already "exploded" (stock up sharply over 3–4 months) on a surge of purchase orders, allegedly from world-model / physical-AI customers. Other supply-chain names have also run.

The goal: **find the suppliers that have NOT yet re-rated** but predictably will, where the incremental world-model spend is large relative to the supplier's existing revenue base — i.e., **revenue *multiplication*, not a marginal bump.** The asymmetry comes from the spend dwarfing the supplier's current revenue.

## Key assumptions to be tested
1. **Demand is real and large:** enough VC + strategic (e.g. Nvidia) capital exists to expect a large ecosystem spend.
2. **Spend is predictable / watchable:** we can reasonably enumerate what these companies will buy, making the thesis verifiable and monitorable.
3. **Attribution holds:** the already-exploded names (VPG et al.) are actually being driven by world-model-company demand (verify, don't assume).
4. **Revenue multiplication is achievable:** for the target names, the addressable spend is large vs. their existing revenue base.

## Decision gate
Only commit compute to full scanners + deep diligence if the initial vetting shows the assumptions hold (or can be repaired into a sound adjacent thesis).
